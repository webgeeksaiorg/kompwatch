# What Are the Live Counters on the Free Snapshot Page?

When you run a free competitor snapshot, you may see one or more animated counters near your results — for example, **"247 snapshots run today"** or **"12 teams tracking this competitor this week."**

## What Do They Show?

The counters are real-time social proof indicators that reflect KompWatch's overall usage:

- **"Snapshots run today"** — the total number of free + account-based snapshots executed across all users since midnight UTC
- **"Teams tracking [competitor]"** — the number of KompWatch accounts actively monitoring the same URL you just analyzed (rounded to the nearest 5 for privacy)

## Are the Numbers Accurate?

Yes. Both counters are backed by live database queries so they reflect actual usage — they are not hardcoded or inflated. The "teams tracking" figure only counts active, paying or free-plan accounts with the URL set as a competitor.

## Why Do You Show This?

Competitive intelligence is most useful when you know you're not the only one watching. Seeing that other teams already monitor the same competitor gives you a signal about which rivals are considered significant across the market — independent of your own judgment.

## What If I Don't See Any Counters?

Counters are part of an A/B experiment. Some visitors see them and some don't — we're measuring their impact on conversion. If you don't see them, your snapshot results are identical in every other way.

## Does Seeing My Competitor Here Mean Others Know I Ran This Snapshot?

No. The counter shows how many accounts track the competitor, not who they are. Your snapshot is anonymous — no account required, and no identity is logged against the URL you submitted.
