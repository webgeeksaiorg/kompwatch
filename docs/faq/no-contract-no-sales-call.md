# No Contract. No Sales Call.

KompWatch is designed to be self-serve from the first visit to first insight — no enterprise agreement, no demo gate, no sales rep required.

## Do I Need to Sign a Contract?

No. There are no annual contracts, multi-year commitments, or legal agreements to review and sign. You subscribe month-to-month (or annually for a discount) through Stripe. Your card is the only thing you need.

## Do I Have to Talk to Sales First?

No. You can sign up, add your first competitor, and start receiving change digests without ever speaking to anyone. Everything is self-serve at [kompwatch.com](https://kompwatch.com).

If you want to talk to someone — to discuss a Team plan, a custom competitor limit, or a billing-by-invoice arrangement — you're welcome to email [support@kompwatch.com](mailto:support@kompwatch.com). But it's never required.

## What Does "Month-to-Month" Mean?

It means your subscription renews monthly with no lock-in period. You can cancel at any time from [Settings → Manage Subscription](https://kompwatch.com/settings) — no notice period, no cancellation fees, no questions asked.

You can also switch to annual billing for a 20% discount. Annual plans have the same cancel-anytime guarantee — cancellation takes effect at the end of your paid annual period and you keep full access until then.

See [Annual Billing](annual-billing.md) for full details.

## How Is This Different From Tools Like Crayon?

Enterprise CI tools typically require:

- A discovery call before you can see pricing
- Weeks or months of sales negotiation
- Annual contracts starting at $25,000+/yr
- Dedicated onboarding and implementation timelines

KompWatch pricing is public. Sign-up takes under 2 minutes. The lowest paid plan is $49/month with no commitment. There is no enterprise sales process because there is no enterprise sales team — the product does the work.

## What If I Need an Invoice or PO for My Finance Team?

Stripe can generate PDF invoices for every charge automatically. If your company requires a purchase order or billing by invoice, see [Billing by Invoice or PO](billing-by-invoice-or-po.md).
