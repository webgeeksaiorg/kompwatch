# I'm Early-Stage With No Clear Competitors Yet — Can I Still Use KompWatch?

Yes — and you might get more value from it than an established team.

Early-stage founders often assume "competitor monitoring" only makes sense once you have named competitors to track. But the most useful competitive intelligence often comes *before* you know who's really competing with you.

---

## What to Monitor When You Don't Have Clear Competitors

### 1. Category Leaders in Your Space

Even if you're disrupting an existing category, monitor the tools your target customers are *currently using*. These are your de facto competitors — even if your product is fundamentally different.

If you're building a CI tool for SMBs, watch Crayon, Klue, and Kompyte's pricing pages. If you're building a project management tool, watch Asana, Linear, and Monday. You'll learn:

- How they position against new entrants
- When they add features that overlap with your roadmap
- How their pricing evolves as you're building

### 2. Aspiration Competitors (Tools You Want Customers to Switch From)

Who do your first prospect calls mention? Whatever tool they're frustrated with — that's a competitor. Add it to KompWatch even if your product doesn't fully overlap yet.

### 3. Adjacent Tools That Might Expand Into Your Space

If you're building in a space where a larger player might pivot to compete, monitor their `/features`, `/blog`, and `/changelog` pages. You want to know *before* the press release.

### 4. Your Own Site

Add your own homepage, pricing page, and key landing pages to KompWatch. It's a low-effort way to catch accidental regressions — broken copy, pricing display bugs, or A/B test variants that got stuck.

See [Monitoring Your Own Website](./monitoring-your-own-website.md).

### 5. Future Competitors (Ones Raising Rounds or Getting Press)

If you're reading about a company that's entering your space — even if they haven't launched — add their landing page. You'll catch the exact moment they publicly reposition or launch.

## How Many Competitors Do I Need to Monitor?

Even **one URL** is enough to start getting value. The Free plan covers 2 URLs with daily snapshots — enough to run a focused competitive watch on your two biggest threats or category leaders.

As you grow and add more competitors, that's when upgrading to Pro (10 URLs, 6-hour snapshots, instant pricing alerts) makes sense.

See [When Should I Upgrade From Free to Pro?](./when-to-upgrade-from-free-to-pro.md)

## What If I Only Have One Real Competitor?

That's completely fine. Monitoring one competitor deeply — their pricing page, features page, blog, and job listings — is more valuable than shallow coverage of ten. KompWatch lets you track up to 4–5 pages per company across your competitor slots.

See [Monitoring Multiple Pages for the Same Competitor](./monitoring-multiple-pages-per-competitor.md).

| Slot | What to Track | Why |
|------|--------------|-----|
| 1 | Category leader's `/pricing` page | Anchors your pricing decisions |
| 2 | Your own `/pricing` page | Catches display bugs and copy drift |
| *(Pro+)* 3 | Category leader's homepage | Catches rebrand, messaging shifts |
| *(Pro+)* 4 | Rising competitor's landing page | Early warning on new entrants |
| *(Pro+)* 5 | Adjacent tool's `/features` page | Expansion signals |

---

**Bottom line:** You don't need a full competitive landscape to start. One URL, daily snapshots, and a weekly digest is enough to stay informed — and to build the competitive awareness habit that pays off later.
