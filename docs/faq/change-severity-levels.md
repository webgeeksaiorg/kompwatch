# Change Severity Levels — What They Mean

Every change CompeteWatch detects is rated **Low**, **Medium**, or **High** severity by AI. This rating helps you quickly focus on the changes that matter most.

## The Three Levels

| Severity | What it signals | Examples |
|----------|----------------|---------|
| **High** | A significant, strategic move by a competitor — likely requires a response | New pricing tier, major feature launch, product rebrand, pricing reduction across all plans |
| **Medium** | A meaningful update worth tracking but not immediately urgent | New blog post announcing a partnership, updated feature descriptions, added testimonials, trial length change |
| **Low** | Minor or cosmetic change — useful for completeness, low action priority | Copy tweaks, updated stock photos, footer link changes, minor layout adjustments |

## How Severity Is Determined

Severity is assigned by Claude AI after comparing the before/after diff of a competitor page. The AI looks at:

- **Business impact** — Does this change affect pricing, feature parity, or competitive positioning?
- **Scope** — Is this a page-wide change or a small isolated edit?
- **Signal strength** — Is this a deliberate announcement or background noise?

Severity is AI-estimated and may occasionally be miscalibrated. If a change is rated lower than expected, you can view the full diff from your [dashboard](https://kompwatch.com/dashboard) to make your own assessment.

## Using Severity to Filter Noise

On the dashboard, changes are displayed with colour-coded severity badges. If you're seeing too much noise:

- Focus on **High** changes first — these are the ones that may require a competitive response
- Use **Medium** as your regular weekly review queue
- **Low** changes are safe to skim or ignore
