# Can KompWatch Monitor How Competitors Appear in AI Search Results?

AI-generated search results (Perplexity, ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews) are becoming a meaningful visibility surface for SaaS companies. Here's what KompWatch tracks today and what's coming for LLM visibility monitoring.

## What Is LLM Visibility?

When someone searches "best competitor intelligence tool" in Perplexity or ChatGPT, the AI generates a response that may name specific products. Which competitors get named — and how they're described — is becoming as strategically important as traditional SEO rankings.

Tools like [LLMrefs](https://llmrefs.com) have emerged specifically to measure this: tracking which brands appear in AI-generated responses across different AI search engines.

## Does KompWatch Track LLM Visibility Today?

Not yet in production — but **LLM visibility tracking is in final testing and shipping soon**. KompWatch currently monitors competitor **websites** — pricing pages, feature pages, blogs, job listings, and landing pages — using a headless browser. The LLM visibility feature (querying AI search engines to measure competitor mentions) is in active QA and will be available to all users on launch.

## What Does KompWatch Track That's Relevant?

KompWatch monitors the inputs to LLM visibility rather than LLM outputs directly:

- **Blog and content changes** — new posts, topic shifts, and keyword targeting that affect how AI search engines learn about a competitor
- **Pricing and feature page copy** — the language competitors use to position themselves, which surfaces in AI-generated comparisons
- **Job listings** — hiring for SEO, AI/ML, or developer relations often signals investment in content and LLM training data strategies

These signals tell you *why* a competitor's LLM visibility might shift before you see it in AI search results.

## How Does This Compare to Dedicated LLM Visibility Tools?

Tools like LLMrefs focus entirely on measuring AI search presence. KompWatch's strength is connecting that signal to what competitors are *doing on their website* — the content changes, pricing shifts, and hiring moves that drive LLM visibility changes. Combining both gives the fullest picture.
