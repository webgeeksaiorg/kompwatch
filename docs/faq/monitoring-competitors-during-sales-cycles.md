# How do I use KompWatch to win competitive sales deals?

Competitive deals move fast. When a prospect mentions they're evaluating a rival, you need current intel — not a six-month-old battlecard. KompWatch keeps your data live so your team is never caught off guard.

## Set up a "deal room" monitoring list

When a deal goes competitive, add the rival's key pages:

- **Pricing page** — catch any last-minute discounts or new tiers they may roll out
- **Feature/product page** — spot new capabilities they might demo
- **Homepage headline** — their current positioning and proof points
- **Changelog or "What's New"** — freshly shipped features they can claim

Use the competitor's name in your KompWatch label so sales reps can filter by deal opponent.

## Get notified before your next call

Set snapshot frequency to every 6 hours (Pro) or hourly (Team) during an active deal cycle. If the competitor pushes a pricing change or a new integration announcement the morning of your demo, you'll know before the call starts.

## Use the digest to prep battlecards

Your weekly or daily digest surfaces the changes KompWatch detected. Paste relevant entries into your battlecard template or CRM. Because each change includes a summary of *what changed* (not just that it changed), you can quickly assess whether it affects your deal.

## Common signals to watch for

| Signal | What it means |
|--------|---------------|
| Pricing page updated | They may be discounting to win the deal — ask your prospect |
| New integration announced | Check if it's relevant to the prospect's stack |
| "Enterprise" tier added | They may be repositioning upmarket |
| Case study or logo added | New social proof — be ready to counter |
| Feature removed or downplayed | Possible weakness — worth probing |

## After the deal closes

Win or lose, keep monitoring. If you win, track what the competitor does next — they may try to poach the customer later. If you lose, monitor for product gaps they exploited and feed that back to your product team.
