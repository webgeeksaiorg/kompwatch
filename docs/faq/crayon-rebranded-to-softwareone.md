# Crayon Is Now SoftwareOne — What Does That Mean for Me?

In April 2026, SoftwareOne completed its $1.4B acquisition of Crayon, the competitive intelligence platform. As of mid-2026, Crayon has been fully rebranded under the SoftwareOne umbrella. If you're a current or former Crayon customer trying to understand what this means, this FAQ covers the key questions.

## What Happened to Crayon?

Crayon was acquired by SoftwareOne — a global IT software and services company — for approximately $1.4 billion. SoftwareOne's core business is IT asset management, software licensing, and enterprise cloud services; competitive intelligence is not their primary product category.

The Crayon brand has been retired and the product is now positioned as part of SoftwareOne's portfolio. Customers renewing post-acquisition are dealing with SoftwareOne's sales and support teams rather than Crayon's original team.

## Is the Product Still the Same?

At launch post-acquisition, the core features (battlecard builder, market intelligence feeds, win/loss tracking) remain available. However, enterprise acquisitions of this type follow a predictable pattern:

- **Short term:** Product remains largely unchanged as integration work happens behind the scenes.
- **Medium term (12–24 months):** Pricing realigns to enterprise tiers, self-serve access is reduced or eliminated, and the product is bundled with SoftwareOne's existing offerings.
- **Long term:** The standalone CI tool may be folded into SoftwareOne's broader platform or deprioritized in favor of integrations with enterprise software stacks.

There is no guarantee the standalone Crayon/SoftwareOne product will continue at its current price point or feature set when your current contract renews.

## Will My Price Change at Renewal?

Enterprise acquisitions almost always result in pricing changes within 12–24 months. SoftwareOne operates at the enterprise level — their typical deal sizes are much larger than Crayon's original SMB and mid-market positioning. If you're a smaller team on a legacy Crayon contract, expect renewal conversations to push you toward higher tiers or bundled packages.

It's worth auditing your renewal date now, before you're in the middle of a renewal cycle with limited time to evaluate alternatives.

## Do I Need to Migrate My Data?

Not immediately. Your Crayon/SoftwareOne account and data remain accessible while your contract is active. If you decide to move to a different tool before renewal, you can export your competitor tracking history and battlecard content through Crayon's existing export functions.

If your contract expires or is not renewed, request a full data export before your access is terminated.
