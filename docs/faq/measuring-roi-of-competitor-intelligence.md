# How do I measure the ROI of competitor monitoring?

Competitor intelligence is often treated as a "nice to have" until someone asks: *what did we actually get from this?* Here's how to measure the return.

## What outcomes to track

**Product decisions informed by competitor data**
Count the number of roadmap items, pricing changes, or feature decisions where competitor signals played a role. Even one avoided mistake (e.g., not building a feature a competitor just shipped) can justify months of subscription cost.

**Sales cycles shortened**
When sales reps have up-to-date battlecards built from KompWatch digests, deal velocity improves. Track win rates on competitive deals before and after adopting CI. A 5–10% improvement in close rate on competitive deals is a common outcome.

**Time saved on manual research**
Estimate how many hours per week your team spent manually checking competitor websites before KompWatch. At $80–150/hr fully-loaded, even 2 hours/week per person adds up to $8,000–15,000/year per team member — far more than a Pro plan.

**Competitive responses launched**
Track how many times your team responded to a competitor move (pricing change, new feature, rebrand) within days vs. weeks. Faster response = more revenue retained.

## A simple ROI formula

```
ROI = (Value of outcomes) / (Annual KompWatch cost) × 100
```

For a 3-person product team spending 2 hrs/week on CI research at $100/hr:

- Hours saved: 3 × 2 × 52 = 312 hrs/year
- Value: 312 × $100 = **$31,200/year**
- KompWatch Pro cost: **$588/year**
- **ROI: ~53x**

Even conservative estimates typically show 10–30x ROI for teams that use the digests regularly.

## How KompWatch makes tracking easier

- **Change history** lets you document when you spotted a competitor move and what action you took
- **Digest archive** provides a timestamped record of competitive intelligence your team received
- **Severity scoring** (LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH) helps you prioritize which changes warranted a response

## What to tell your manager or CFO

The clearest business case: *"We spend X hours/week on competitor research today. KompWatch automates 80% of that, pays for itself in [N] days, and means we never miss a pricing change or major feature launch again."*

For larger teams considering the Team plan, the math gets even more favorable — centralized digests eliminate duplicated research across product, marketing, and sales.
