# What Does It Mean When a Competitor Goes Quiet?

Most teams focus on catching competitors *doing things* — launching features, dropping prices, adding case studies. But a competitor that **stops making changes** is often signaling something just as important.

---

## What "Going Quiet" Looks Like

KompWatch tracks change velocity — how often a competitor's monitored pages change. Signs of a competitor going quiet:

- **Pricing page**: No changes in 8+ weeks (especially after a period of frequent updates)
- **Changelog / "What's New"**: Releases that used to be monthly drop to quarterly or stop entirely
- **Features page**: Stagnant — same capability list for months
- **Careers page**: Job listings disappear entirely or roles stay unfilled for 90+ days
- **Blog / content**: Posts dry up after consistent publishing

If you see multiple pages all going quiet at the same time, that's a meaningful cluster signal.

---

## What Going Quiet Usually Means

### Scenario 1: Pivoting or repositioning
Teams that are rethinking their product direction often stop iterating on their current offering. The existing site becomes a holding pattern while internal work happens. Expect a refresh or repositioning announcement in 3–6 months.

### Scenario 2: Funding or acquisition pressure
Companies running out of runway reduce marketing output. Engineering gets focused on survival features, not launches. Going quiet after a period of high activity can precede a down round, acqui-hire, or shutdown.

### Scenario 3: Going enterprise
If a competitor is moving upmarket, they often strip their public-facing pricing page ("Contact Sales"), reduce self-serve content, and stop the product changelog (enterprise buyers don't want to see rapid iteration). This is actually a *strategic opportunity* — their SMB/mid-market users become available.

### Scenario 4: They've already shipped the thing
Sometimes quiet means they've launched and moved into scale mode. Less visible iteration doesn't always mean trouble.

## How to Read the Hiring Correlation

The most reliable signal combination is:

**Pricing/features page going quiet + careers page going quiet simultaneously**

This is different from:

**Pricing/features page going quiet + careers page surging with new ML/infra roles**

The second pattern usually means a large under-the-hood rebuild is underway. Expect a major release or rebrand within 6 months.

## Using KompWatch to Track Change Velocity

KompWatch's **Activity Score** (Pro and Team plans) measures change velocity per competitor over the past 30 days. A competitor whose activity score drops sharply is worth flagging in your next competitive review.

You can also:
- Filter your **Changes** timeline by competitor to spot gaps in activity
- Sort competitors in the dashboard by "Last Change" to quickly see who has gone dark
- Set up a weekly review cadence where a *lack* of changes is a standing agenda item — not just new changes

---

## When to Act on a Quiet Competitor

Going quiet isn't always actionable immediately, but it's a prompt to:

1. **Check their social channels and G2/Capterra reviews** — are customers noticing anything?
2. **Ask your sales team** — are displaced customers reaching out?
3. **Monitor their job board harder** — what they're hiring (or not hiring) tells you direction
4. **Update your battlecard** — if they're in enterprise transition, your SMB positioning becomes stronger

The absence of change is data. KompWatch gives you the history to spot it.
