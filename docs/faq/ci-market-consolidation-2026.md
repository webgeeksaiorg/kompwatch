# What's Happening to the Competitive Intelligence Market in 2026?

The competitive intelligence software market went through significant structural changes in 2025–2026: two major acquisitions and multiple layoff rounds at the largest vendors. If you're evaluating CI tools — or reconsidering your current vendor — here's a plain-English summary of what happened and what it means.

## What Happened

**Kompyte → acquired by Adobe (2025)**
Adobe acquired Kompyte and folded it into the Adobe Experience Cloud suite. Kompyte is no longer a standalone self-serve product. Teams that bought Kompyte for lightweight monitoring now need to go through Adobe enterprise sales to renew.

**Crayon → acquired by SoftwareOne ($1.4B, April 2026)**
SoftwareOne — an enterprise IT and software licensing company — acquired Crayon for $1.4B. SoftwareOne is not a product-led growth company; its core business is enterprise software procurement and IT management. The acquisition adds an enterprise ownership layer to a product that was already priced at $25K–$40K/yr.

**Klue — two significant layoff rounds (2025 and May 2026)**
Klue cut approximately 40% of staff in 2025, then laid off another 100 employees in May 2026 — roughly a third of their remaining team — citing a "focus on AI." Their CEO publicly acknowledged losing deals to ChatGPT. The product direction is shifting toward Microsoft stack integration (Teams, Dynamics 365, Copilot MCP Server) and AI-restructured workflows.

## What This Means for CI Buyers

**The enterprise consolidation trend:** The three largest dedicated CI platforms have all experienced significant structural changes in under 18 months. Crayon and Kompyte are now owned by enterprise IT companies with very different go-to-market approaches. Klue is restructuring around a narrower AI/Microsoft bet.

**The commoditization signal:** Klue's CEO naming ChatGPT as a competitive threat is meaningful. The "summarize what a competitor does" layer — historically a core CI platform value — is increasingly available through general-purpose AI at no additional cost. What remains defensible is *real-time monitoring*: detecting when something actually changes on a competitor's website, pricing page, or product documentation.

**What's not changing:** None of these vendors are shutting down. Your contract remains valid through its current term. Existing data is not at risk. The concern is longer-term: slower product development, pricing pressure at renewal, and roadmap shifts that may not align with your use case.

## Who This Affects Most

- **Teams mid-contract with Crayon or Kompyte** — nothing changes immediately, but evaluate before your next renewal.
- **Klue customers on Microsoft stack** — the platform may be doubling down in a direction that works well for you.
- **Klue customers not on Microsoft stack** — the new features being built (Teams, Dynamics 365 integration) may not apply to your workflow. Renewal is the right time to audit whether the platform still fits.
- **Teams evaluating CI tools for the first time** — this is a good moment to differentiate between full CI platforms (battlecards, win/loss, CRM integration) and monitoring-focused tools (website change detection, AI digests, Slack alerts). The use cases are different and the pricing reflects that.
