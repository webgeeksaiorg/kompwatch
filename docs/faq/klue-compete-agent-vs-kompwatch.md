# Klue Compete Agent vs KompWatch — What's the Difference?

Klue and KompWatch solve adjacent but fundamentally different problems. This article explains the distinction so you can choose the right tool — or decide whether you need both.

---

## What Is Klue Compete Agent?

Klue's Compete Agent is an AI-powered competitive intelligence layer that surfaces curated competitor information inside your team's workflow tools — primarily Microsoft Teams, Teams Calls, and Dynamics 365 CRM. It is designed around the idea that salespeople shouldn't have to leave their existing tools to access competitive context: the agent brings intel to them, inline, during calls and in chat.

Core use cases for Compete Agent:
- **In-call intelligence** — surfaces competitor talking points during a live Teams call based on what's being discussed
- **CRM-embedded battlecards** — delivers competitive context inside Dynamics 365 deal records
- **AI Q&A** — answers "what are competitor X's key weaknesses?" from a curated intelligence corpus
- **Microsoft Copilot integration** — exposes competitive data via Copilot MCP Server

Klue Compete Agent is optimized for **consuming and distributing** competitive intelligence, assuming that intelligence has already been gathered.

---

## What Is KompWatch?

KompWatch is an automated website monitoring tool that tells you when competitors *change* their public web presence — pricing, features, messaging, hiring, legal. It runs continuously in the background, takes regular snapshots of competitor pages, and uses AI to detect and explain what changed.

Core use cases for KompWatch:
- **Change detection** — catch pricing updates, new feature announcements, positioning shifts, and hiring surges the moment they happen
- **AI digests** — receive plain-English summaries of what changed and why it might matter
- **Alert routing** — push critical changes to Slack, Teams, or email as they're detected
- **Battlecard generation** — export one-click battlecards from live change data

KompWatch is optimized for **detecting** competitive changes in real time — the raw material that feeds any CI workflow.

## Who Should Use Each?

**Use Klue Compete Agent if:**
- Your team is deeply embedded in Microsoft Teams and Dynamics 365
- You primarily need a battlecard delivery mechanism and in-call AI assistance
- You have a CI curator or analyst who maintains the intelligence corpus
- You want AI to answer "what do competitors do?" questions inside your workflow

**Use KompWatch if:**
- You want to be alerted the moment a competitor changes their pricing, features, or messaging — without analyst overhead
- You're a small team (1–50 people) without a dedicated CI function
- You aren't on the Microsoft stack
- You want to start free and scale gradually
- Your primary question is "what changed?" not "what is?"

**Use both if:**
- Your CI team maintains Klue for internal distribution and battlecard management, but needs a reliable change detection feed to keep that corpus current. KompWatch can serve as the monitoring layer that triggers Klue corpus updates.

---

## Are They Direct Competitors?

Partially. Where they overlap:
- Both position around competitive intelligence
- Both use AI to surface and explain competitor information
- Both integrate with team communication tools

Where they don't:
- KompWatch detects *changes to live websites*. Klue Compete Agent answers questions about *known competitor information*. These are complementary data layers, not substitutes.
- KompWatch requires no analyst to operate. Klue requires curation to keep the intelligence corpus useful.
- KompWatch works for any team regardless of tech stack. Klue Compete Agent is built around Microsoft's ecosystem.
