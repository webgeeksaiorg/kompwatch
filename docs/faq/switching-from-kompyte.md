## How to Switch

Setup is fast — most teams are monitoring within 10 minutes.

1. **Sign up at [kompwatch.com](https://kompwatch.com)** — free, no credit card required.
2. **Add the same competitor URLs** you were tracking in Kompyte. See [How to Add a Competitor](./adding-competitors.md) for steps.
3. **Set CSS selectors** to focus on specific page sections (e.g. `.pricing-table`, `#features-section`). This reduces noise from navigation and footer updates.
4. **Configure your alert preferences** in Settings → Notifications. KompWatch consolidates changes into daily digests (Pro) or weekly digests (Free) rather than firing per-change alerts.

Your first snapshot runs within a few minutes of adding a competitor. Change detection begins with the second snapshot.

## Will I Lose My Kompyte History?

KompWatch does not import historical data from Kompyte. Your monitoring history restarts from the moment you add a competitor. If you're on an active Kompyte contract, consider timing your switch at the end of your billing cycle.

## Running Both at the Same Time

KompWatch's free tier (2 competitors) lets you run parallel monitoring alongside Kompyte for a month before fully switching. This is the lowest-risk way to compare digest quality and coverage.

## What KompWatch Doesn't Have (Yet)

KompWatch focuses on automated website monitoring — pricing pages, feature copy, blog posts, job listings, tech stack signals. A few things Kompyte offers that KompWatch doesn't (yet):

- **Battlecards** — on KompWatch's roadmap, not yet shipped
- **Win/loss analysis** — not currently available
- **CRM sync** — KompWatch sends alerts via email, Slack, and webhook; no native CRM integration yet

If automated monitoring with AI digests is your primary use case (and for most teams, it is), KompWatch covers it at a fraction of the cost. If you rely heavily on battlecard workflows or CRM-integrated win/loss reporting, check back — those are planned features.
