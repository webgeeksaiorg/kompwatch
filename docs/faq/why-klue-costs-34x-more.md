# Why Is KompWatch 34× Cheaper Than Klue?

The /vs-klue comparison page shows a 34× price premium for Klue's Gartner Magic Quadrant recognition. Here is exactly how that number is calculated — and an honest take on whether the comparison is fair.

## The Math

| | KompWatch Pro | Klue (entry) |
|---|---|---|
| Annual cost | $588/yr ($49 × 12) | ~$20,000/yr |
| Per-seat basis | Flat (all users) | Per seat (varies) |
| Contract | Monthly, cancel anytime | Annual minimum |

$20,000 ÷ $588 = **34×**

That is the entry-level Klue ACV against KompWatch's Pro plan. Klue's actual median ACV for mid-market teams is typically higher — independent reviews put it in the $25K–$40K range depending on seat count and contract length.

## Is the Comparison Fair?

Partly — and we think it's worth being honest about this.

**Where the comparison is fair:**
- Both products track competitor websites and surface changes
- Both deliver AI-generated summaries of what changed
- The core "know when a competitor updates their pricing page" job is done by both

**Where the comparison undersells Klue:**
- Klue includes dedicated CSM onboarding, quarterly business reviews, and a customer success team
- Klue's battlecard CMS supports 50+ rep sales orgs distributing intel at scale
- Klue has SOC 2 Type II, ISO 27001, and enterprise SSO — KompWatch is still working toward SOC 2
- Klue's Compete Agent integrates directly into Microsoft Teams Calls and Dynamics 365
- Klue has formal win/loss program infrastructure built in

**Where the comparison favors KompWatch:**
- KompWatch requires no onboarding — you're tracking competitors in 2 minutes, not 8–15 weeks
- KompWatch is fully automated — no curator needed to get value
- KompWatch is monthly, cancel anytime — no $20K commitment to discover whether CI fits your workflow
- KompWatch's roadmap is built for 1–20 person teams, not Fortune 500 sales orgs

## What You're Actually Paying For

The 34× premium buys you:

1. **Enterprise procurement credibility** — Gartner recognition that satisfies IT and Legal sign-off processes
2. **Dedicated human support** — CSM, onboarding, and ongoing success management
3. **Enterprise compliance** — SOC 2, ISO 27001, BAA availability, DPA support
4. **Battlecard distribution infrastructure** — built for sales orgs with 50+ reps
5. **Deep CRM/productivity integrations** — Salesforce, Microsoft 365, Dynamics 365, Copilot

If your team needs all five of those things, Klue is probably the right tool and the premium is justified.

If your team needs "automated alerts when competitors change + plain-English summaries + Slack notifications," you are paying the 34× premium for features you will not use.

We built the 34× stat into the comparison page because it's a real number, not a cherry-picked extreme. We could have compared against Klue's highest-tier pricing to make the gap look even larger, or compared against KompWatch's free tier to inflate it further.
