# What Should I Do When My Competitive Intelligence Vendor Has Layoffs?

Layoffs at a CI vendor don't mean the product is going away tomorrow — but they're a useful forcing function to audit whether you're still getting value from your investment. This article covers what layoffs typically signal and how to think through your options.

## Is My Data Safe?

In the short term: yes. SaaS companies that do layoffs typically continue operating — they're cutting costs, not shutting down. Your data remains accessible, monitoring continues, and your contract is still valid.

The risk isn't data loss. The risk is slower product development, support degradation, and pricing pressure at renewal.

## What Layoffs Usually Signal

Not all layoffs are the same. Context matters:

**"Focus on AI" restructurings** — A company cutting generalist headcount to rebuild around AI (the framing Klue used for its May 2026 round) is placing a product bet. The product direction may shift significantly. Features you rely on might be deprioritized; new features may not match your workflow.

**Cost-cutting layoffs** — Often precede pricing changes. The company is reducing burn; price increases at renewal are common within 6–12 months.

**Post-acquisition restructuring** — If the vendor was recently acquired (see [When Your CI Vendor Gets Acquired](./when-your-ci-vendor-gets-acquired.md)), layoffs may be part of consolidation into the parent platform.

**Signal that matters most:** Two or more significant layoff rounds in under 18 months. That pattern indicates structural instability, not routine optimization.

## Questions to Ask Before Your Next Renewal

1. Is the team that built the features I use still there?
2. Has the product roadmap changed in a direction that no longer serves my use case?
3. Has support quality degraded since the layoffs?
4. Is pricing likely to change at renewal?
5. Am I paying for capabilities I use — or a platform I've outgrown?

You don't need to act immediately. But renewal is the right time to have this conversation — not after you've auto-renewed for another year.

## Klue Specifically (2025–2026 Layoffs)

Klue has had two significant rounds of cuts. In 2025, approximately 85 employees were let go — around 40% of staff at the time — as part of an earlier restructuring. In May 2026, a further ~100 employees were laid off (roughly a third of the remaining team), citing a "focus on AI." Their CEO acknowledged losing deals to ChatGPT, which suggests a portion of Klue's value proposition has been commoditized by general-purpose AI.

Klue is not shutting down. But if you're a current Klue customer:

- **Your contract is valid through its current term** — nothing changes immediately.
- **The product direction is shifting** toward Microsoft stack integration (Teams, Dynamics 365, Copilot) and AI-restructured workflows. If your team isn't on the Microsoft enterprise stack, the new features being built may not apply to you.
- **Renewal is the moment to audit** whether you're using the platform capabilities you're paying for, or whether a lighter-weight tool would cover your actual use case at lower cost.
