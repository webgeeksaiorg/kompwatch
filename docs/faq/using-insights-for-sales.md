# Using KompWatch Insights for Sales and Competitive Deals

KompWatch monitors competitor websites automatically and delivers AI-generated summaries to your inbox. This article explains how to route those insights to your sales team and use them in active deals.

## Getting Alerts to Your Sales Team

The fastest way to get competitor changes in front of reps is via **Slack**. Set up a `#competitive-intel` channel and connect it in **Settings → Integrations → Slack**. Every new change above your severity threshold posts there automatically.

For deal-specific intel, reps can filter by competitor on the [dashboard](https://kompwatch.com/dashboard) — useful the night before a call with a prospect who's evaluating that competitor.

## Reading the Digest as a Sales Brief

Every digest entry includes three fields that double as competitive talking points:

| Field | What it is | How to use it in a call |
|-------|-----------|------------------------|
| **Change type** | Pricing / Feature / Blog / Job / Tech | Quickly classify whether this is positioning ammo or just noise |
| **Summary** | Plain-English description of the change | Read this before the call — know what changed without visiting the page |
| **What this means for you** | AI-generated strategic implication | This is your talking point. Quote it directly or riff on it. |

The **"What this means for you"** line is generated by Claude specifically to translate raw changes into competitive positioning. For example:

> **What this means for you:** Competitor just removed their free tier — prospects who relied on that plan are now in the market. A strong moment to lead with KompWatch's free plan in your next outreach.

You don't need to interpret the change — the AI does it. Your job is to act on it.

## Setting Up Deal-Based Monitoring

If a prospect mentions they're also evaluating Acme Corp, add Acme to your tracked competitors immediately. Set the CSS selector to their pricing page if pricing is the battleground:

1. Go to [kompwatch.com/competitors](https://kompwatch.com/competitors) → **Add competitor**
2. Enter Acme's URL and set the CSS selector to `.pricing` or `#pricing-section` if you know it, or leave it as `body` for full-page monitoring
3. Monitoring starts within the hour — you'll see any changes before your next call

See [Adding a Competitor](./adding-competitors.md) and [CSS Selector Targeting](./css-selectors.md) for setup details.

## Useful Signals to Watch Before a Sales Call

| Signal type | What to look for | What it means for the deal |
|-------------|-----------------|---------------------------|
| Pricing change | Price increase, tier removal, plan rename | Prospect may have cost anxiety — lead with your pricing stability or free trial |
| New feature | New capability on their features page | Know what you're being compared to — have a response ready |
| Job listings | Surge in sales or ML hiring | Competitor is scaling fast — prospect may hear about them more |
| Blog post | "Enterprise", "security", "compliance" in titles | Competitor is repositioning — find the positioning gap |

Job listing tracking is covered in detail at [Reading Competitor Job Listing Signals](./reading-competitor-job-listing-signals.md).

## Routing High-Severity Changes to Sales Leadership

Critical and High severity changes — pricing overhauls, major feature launches, acquisition signals — may warrant a direct Slack message or email to the sales team lead, not just a channel post. You can configure this in **Settings → Notifications → Alert routing**.

For custom routing or webhook integration with your CRM or sales tool, see [Integrations and Notifications](./integrations-and-notifications.md).

## Building Informal Battlecards

KompWatch doesn't generate battlecards automatically (it's on the roadmap), but you can build a running battlecard from your digest history:

1. Go to [kompwatch.com/competitors/[id]](https://kompwatch.com/competitors) and open any tracked competitor
2. Browse the **change history** — High and Critical changes are the battlecard material
3. Export via **Settings → Export → JSON** and paste the summaries into your battlecard template

See [Exporting Your Data](./exporting-your-data.md) for export options.

## What KompWatch Does Not Replace

KompWatch monitors website changes — it does not capture win/loss data, record sales call transcripts, or sync to Salesforce. If your team needs a full win/loss workflow with CRM integration, KompWatch is the monitoring layer that feeds it.

For how KompWatch compares to battlecard tools and full CI platforms, see [How KompWatch Compares to Alternatives](./comparing-to-alternatives.md).
