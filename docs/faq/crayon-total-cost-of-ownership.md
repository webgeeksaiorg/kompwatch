# What Is the Real Total Cost of Crayon? (Beyond the License Fee)

Crayon's listed price is $25K–$40K/yr based on Vendr 2026 data. But the license is only part of the cost. Teams that have run the full math typically arrive at $50K–$80K/yr when you include the labor required to get value from the platform.

## The Three Cost Categories

### 1. License Fee
- **Crayon:** $25,000–$40,000/yr (annual contract, sales-negotiated)
- **KompWatch Pro:** $588/yr ($49/mo × 12)

### 2. Setup and Onboarding Labor
Crayon requires a structured onboarding process: configuring the platform, integrating with Salesforce or HubSpot, building battlecard templates, and training the team on the workflow. Based on customer reviews (G2, Gartner Peer Insights), this typically takes:

- **2–4 weeks** of a product marketing manager or CI manager's time
- Equivalent to **$4,000–$10,000** in loaded labor cost at typical PMM salaries ($80K–$120K/yr)

KompWatch targets 15 minutes to first snapshot, no onboarding required.

### 3. Ongoing Maintenance Labor
This is the largest hidden cost and the one most frequently cited in Crayon's G2 reviews.

Crayon surfaces raw competitive signals — pricing changes, feature announcements, press coverage — but synthesizing those signals into usable intelligence requires human analyst hours:

- **Battlecard maintenance:** Battlecards go stale within weeks of a competitor update. Keeping 5–10 battlecards current requires regular review and revision — estimated at 2–4 hours/week for a CI-focused role.
- **Curation and triage:** Crayon generates high signal volume. Teams report spending significant time filtering noise from actionable changes.
- **Stakeholder distribution:** Building and distributing competitive newsletters or Slack digests to sales and product teams requires additional coordination.

At 2 hours/week of a PMM's time ($80K/yr base = ~$40/hr loaded), that's roughly **$4,200/yr in ongoing maintenance labor** — minimum. Teams that run a true CI program with dedicated analyst hours report 5–10+ hours/week, pushing annual labor costs to **$20,000–$40,000**.

## Why the Labor Cost Is Real

Crayon is a platform, not a fully automated service. It aggregates signals and provides tools to build battlecards and win/loss reports — but the analysis, curation, and distribution still require human judgment. That's the right tradeoff for large CI teams with dedicated analyst roles.

For teams that want automated monitoring and AI-generated change summaries without the analyst overhead, that model doesn't fit.

## Who Should Still Consider Crayon

KompWatch is not a replacement for Crayon's full feature set. Crayon is worth the cost if:

- You have a dedicated CI manager or team whose primary job is competitive strategy
- You need native battlecard generation with Salesforce or HubSpot sync
- Win/loss tracking and deal review workflows are central to your sales process
- You're managing a large competitive landscape (50+ competitors) with multi-stakeholder distribution requirements

If those requirements don't describe your team — and your core need is "tell me when competitor websites change" — the $25K–$40K license (plus labor) is difficult to justify.
