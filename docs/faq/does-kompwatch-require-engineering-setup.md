# Does KompWatch Require Engineering Setup?

No. KompWatch requires zero engineering work to get started. A PM, marketer, or founder can set it up in under two minutes — no tickets filed, no sprint capacity borrowed, no vendor kickoff calls.

## What setup looks like

1. **Sign up** — email + magic link, 30 seconds. No sales call, no credit card required.
2. **Paste competitor URLs** — add the pages you want to monitor. Pick a CSS selector to target a specific section, or leave it blank to monitor the whole page.
3. **Get your first digest** — AI-generated change summaries land in your inbox on your next scheduled cycle. Done.

No engineering integration is involved at any step.

## What you do NOT need

- No Salesforce connector or CRM integration
- No SSO/SAML configuration (available on Team plan, but not required)
- No API keys or SDK installation
- No webhook plumbing (Slack alerts are optional — one OAuth click if you want them)
- No dedicated onboarding manager, kickoff call, or training sessions
- No annual contract

## How this compares to enterprise CI tools

Enterprise platforms like Crayon require 8–15 weeks to go live: sales call, legal/procurement, implementation kickoff, engineering integration (Salesforce connector, SSO, webhook setup), competitor configuration, and team training. KompWatch skips the entire enterprise onboarding track.

## Optional technical features

A few KompWatch features are *optionally* technical if you want to use them:

| Feature | Technical? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| CSS selector targeting | Optional | Improves signal quality; not required. Any developer-familiar person can write `.pricing-table` or `#features`. |
| Slack / webhook alerts | Optional | Slack is one OAuth click. Custom webhooks require a URL from your receiving service. |
| CSV / JSON export | No | Self-serve from the dashboard. |
| API access | Team plan | For teams building internal tooling on top of KompWatch data. |

## Who typically sets up KompWatch

Most KompWatch accounts are set up by a product manager, growth marketer, or founder — not an engineer. The free plan (2 competitors, no credit card) means you can verify it works for your use case before involving anyone else.
