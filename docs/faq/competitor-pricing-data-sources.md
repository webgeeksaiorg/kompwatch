# Where Does KompWatch Get Competitor Pricing Data?

The pricing comparisons on our [pricing page](https://kompwatch.com/pricing) reference figures like "$28,750/yr" for enterprise CI platforms (Crayon, Klue). Here's where those numbers come from and how to evaluate them.

## Our Sources

**Vendr / Vendr Benchmarks** — Vendr aggregates anonymized SaaS transaction data from thousands of B2B purchases. Their 2026 benchmark data shows median Crayon contracts at $28,750/yr and Klue contracts in the $20K–$40K range. These are the most reliable publicly available figures for enterprise CI tools.

**G2 Buyer Intent & Review Data** — G2 collects self-reported pricing from reviewers. Crayon reviews consistently cite five-figure annual contracts. We cross-reference G2 against Vendr to spot outliers.

**Public Pricing Pages** — Where competitors publish pricing directly (RivalSense, Changeflow, Seeto, Caelian), we source from their pricing pages and note the date reviewed.

**Procurement disclosures** — Some SaaS tools publish or share redacted contract ranges in public pitch decks, investor memos, or media coverage. We incorporate these where available.

## Caveats

- **Enterprise prices are negotiated.** The $28,750/yr figure is a median, not a floor or ceiling. Smaller teams may pay less; larger enterprises may pay significantly more.
- **Prices change.** We review competitor pricing quarterly. If you see a figure that seems off, [email us](mailto:support@kompwatch.com) and we'll verify.
- **We're biased.** We publish these comparisons to make the case for KompWatch. Read them critically and do your own diligence — most of these tools have free trials or will share pricing on a call.
