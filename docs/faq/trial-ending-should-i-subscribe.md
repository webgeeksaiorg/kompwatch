# My Trial Is Ending — Should I Subscribe?

If your 7-day Pro trial is winding down and you're not sure whether to add a card, this page is for you. Here's an honest framework for making the call.

---

## The Honest Short Answer

If you added real competitors and got at least one change alert — even a minor one — the infrastructure is working. You haven't missed anything important yet. The question isn't whether the trial wowed you in 7 days; it's whether you want someone watching your competitors for the next year.

---

## Three Questions to Ask Yourself

### 1. Did I set it up correctly?

The trial only shows value if you gave it real input. Run through this checklist:

| Setup step | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Added 3+ real competitors (not test URLs) | More monitored = more likely to catch a move during trial week |
| Added specific pages per competitor (`/pricing`, `/features`, `/changelog`) | Homepage-only monitoring misses the high-signal pages |
| Waited at least 48 hours for multiple snapshot cycles | First snapshot is the baseline — changes only show from the second snapshot onward |
| Checked the digest email (not just the dashboard) | Digest is the core product experience; the raw feed is secondary |

If you only added one competitor, only monitored the homepage, and checked it after 24 hours — you didn't evaluate the product. You can [extend your trial](#can-i-get-more-time) and do it properly.

---

### 2. Was the trial quiet because your competitors didn't change — or because the setup was incomplete?

A quiet week can mean two very different things:

**A) Your competitors genuinely didn't change anything meaningful.** Pricing pages, feature lists, and homepages commonly go 4–12 weeks without a significant update. Quiet trial ≠ broken monitoring. KompWatch was watching; your competitors just didn't move.

**B) The setup was incomplete.** If you only monitored the homepage, or only added 1 competitor, or your snapshots had an error, you may have missed changes that were there to catch.

Go to **Competitors → [competitor name]** and check the Snapshot History tab. If snapshots ran successfully on the right pages, the monitoring worked — you just had a quiet week.

---

### 3. What's the real cost of *not* knowing?

Think about the last time a competitor made a move — pricing cut, new feature announcement, repositioning. How long did it take you to find out?

If the answer is "days to weeks, via a sales call" — that's the gap KompWatch closes. $49/month is roughly **$1.60/day** to know within 6 hours of a competitor pricing page change, rather than days after the fact.

The cost of missing one competitive pricing change in a deal is typically 20–100× the monthly subscription.

### "I barely got any alerts. I'm not sure it's working."

### "I'm not sure my competitors' sites are dynamic enough."

KompWatch handles JavaScript-rendered (SPA/React) pages using headless Chromium — it doesn't just diff raw HTML. Even sites that load content dynamically are monitored correctly. See [Monitoring JavaScript SPA Sites →](./monitoring-javascript-spa-sites.md)

Or send your team a link to the [demo competitor](./demo-competitor-acme-analytics.md) or the [sample digest preview](./sample-digest-preview.md) — both are shareable without needing an account.

### "I can get by with Google Alerts and manual checks for now."

You can — many teams do. The question is what it costs in time and missed changes. See [Google Alerts Alongside KompWatch →](./google-alerts-alongside-kompwatch.md) for an honest comparison. If you're spending 2+ hours/month on manual checks, KompWatch is almost certainly cheaper on a time-cost basis.

## Bottom Line

If you set it up correctly and had a quiet week — subscribe. You're paying $49/month for the alert you *won't* miss, not the ones you already got. The value of competitor monitoring is invisible when everything is stable and very visible the moment something changes.

If the setup was incomplete — ask for an extension and give it a proper week.

If it's genuinely not the right fit — let it expire and stay on the Free plan (2 competitors, daily snapshots). No hard feelings and no charges.
