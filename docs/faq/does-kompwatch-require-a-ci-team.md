# Does KompWatch Require a Dedicated CI Team?

No. KompWatch is built for teams that do not have — and do not want to hire — a dedicated competitive intelligence function.

A single person (founder, PM, marketer, or sales lead) can set up KompWatch, receive AI-generated change summaries, and act on competitor moves without any specialist knowledge or ongoing analyst overhead.

## What "No CI team required" means in practice

Enterprise CI platforms like Crayon and Klue are designed around a **human-in-the-loop** model. They surface raw signals — pricing changes, news hits, review site activity, social mentions — and rely on a dedicated CI analyst or competitive intelligence manager to:

- Review and filter signals for relevance
- Write and maintain battlecards
- Produce stakeholder reports
- Manage false positives
- Onboard new competitors into the system

This work is real: roughly 10–20 hours per week for a properly staffed CI function. For teams without that headcount, the platform sits underused or the job gets split across whoever has a spare hour.

KompWatch is designed to skip the curation layer entirely:

| Task | Enterprise CI approach | KompWatch |
|---|---|---|
| Signal filtering | Analyst reviews raw signals | AI classifies by type and severity |
| Change summaries | Analyst writes interpretation | AI generates plain-English digest |
| Battlecard updates | Analyst curates and publishes | JSON export + (battlecard generation in review) |
| Competitor onboarding | Analyst builds profile, adds pages | Self-serve — paste URL, done in 60 seconds |
| Stakeholder distribution | Analyst pulls weekly report | Digest emailed automatically to your inbox |
| False positive management | Analyst dismisses low-quality signals | Severity filter (LOW/MEDIUM/HIGH) + change-type filter |

## Who typically runs KompWatch

Most KompWatch accounts are owned by one person — usually whoever has the highest competitive intelligence need:

- **Founder** — tracking 2–5 direct competitors during early growth
- **Product manager** — monitoring feature and pricing changes that affect roadmap decisions
- **Growth marketer** — tracking messaging and positioning shifts across competitor sites
- **Sales lead** — getting early signals on competitor pricing moves and new feature launches

These users spend roughly 10–15 minutes per week reviewing their digest. They don't file tickets, run reports, or maintain battlecards.

## When you might eventually want a CI function

KompWatch doesn't require a CI team, but it also doesn't replace one at enterprise scale. If your situation includes any of the following, a dedicated CI analyst adds value that KompWatch alone can't provide:

- A sales team of 50+ AEs where battlecard quality directly affects $1M+ deal cycles
- Board-level competitive reporting that requires human strategic narrative
- A complex analyst workflow feeding Salesforce opportunity data and win/loss attribution
- Competitors operating in channels (closed communities, analyst relations, private events) that can't be monitored by web scraping

For these needs, Klue or Crayon — plus a CI analyst — is the right investment. KompWatch is often used as the *monitoring layer* that feeds into a human curator, rather than replacing the curator entirely.

## Summary

If you're a startup, growth-stage team, or any organization without a dedicated CI budget, you don't need a CI team to get value from KompWatch. Setup takes under two minutes. Your first digest arrives the same day. No analyst, no specialist, no overhead.
