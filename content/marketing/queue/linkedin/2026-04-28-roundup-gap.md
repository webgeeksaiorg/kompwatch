Searched "best Crayon alternatives 2026" this morning.

Eight results. All recommending the same five tools: Klue, Kompyte, RivalSense, Similarweb, SpyFu. Cheapest entry point across all of them: $99/month.

KompWatch isn't in any of them. Neither is any other tool under $100/month.

That's not a complaint — it's a timing problem. Those roundups are mostly written by the tools themselves or SEO shops with paid placements. The "best alternatives" lists don't find the most affordable alternative. They find whatever has the review count and the SEO budget to get listed.

Real discovery for a pre-launch SaaS happens differently: direct search, someone mentioning it in a Slack, Indie Hackers, word of mouth from an early user.

The roundup game takes 6-12 months. You get listed after reviews, after case studies, after G2 has enough ratings to include you. It's not unfair. It's just the sequence.

Month one problem. Working the channels that actually move in month one.

(If you want to see what the affordable alternative actually looks like: kompwatch.com — competitor monitoring for teams that don't have a $25K/yr budget.)
