This week in KompWatch — the things I actually noticed:

**Crayon got acquired by SoftwareOne for $1.4B.** I've been getting more inbound from Crayon customers in the last two weeks than the previous two months combined. People are mid-contract and suddenly uncertain. I don't have a playbook for this yet. Just taking calls.

**Shipped mobile email on Thursday.** The digest email now renders properly on phones. Sounds small. One beta tester texted me "finally readable" at 7am and I felt unreasonably proud about it. Mobile formatting has been broken since day one and I kept deprioritizing it. Shipped it in 4 hours when I finally stopped avoiding it.

**The scraper now handles session-authenticated competitor pages.** Some pricing pages are behind "request a demo" gates. Built a session replay approach — pre-authenticate once, replay the session each cycle. It works. Still sitting with the ethics of it. Rate-limited hard, robots.txt respected. Still.

**The thing I keep learning:** Competitors don't change things on Monday morning. They change things on Wednesday afternoon when product teams are in planning. The gap between when a change happens and when a team notices is usually 1-3 weeks. That's the window that matters.
