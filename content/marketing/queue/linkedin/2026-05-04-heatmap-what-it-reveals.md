Shipped a GitHub-style activity heatmap for competitor changes today.

26 weeks. One square per day. Color intensity = number of detected changes.

I expected it to be a nice-to-have visual. What I didn't expect: it makes competitor release cadence immediately obvious.

You can see: three weeks of heavy activity → quiet → three more weeks → quiet. That's a competitor on a sprint cycle. You can see when they went silent for two months, then lit up right before their pricing announcement. That's a company in pre-launch mode.

A weekly email digest doesn't show you this. A list of alerts definitely doesn't. You only see the pattern when you can see the shape of the data over time.

The insight isn't just "what changed." It's "when they're in motion."

That's harder to miss than a buried Tuesday email that said "competitor updated their pricing page."

Still building this solo. Still figuring out the right way to surface these patterns automatically, not just visually. But the heatmap was the first time I looked at a competitor's data and thought: huh, I know something about how they operate now.
