Your team is probably spending 65 hours/year reading competitor changelogs manually.

5 competitors × 15-20 minutes × weekly = 65 hours of Friday tab-switching. No memory of what changed last week vs. this week. No diff. Just vibes and notes in a doc nobody revisits.

I built KompWatch because I was doing this exact thing. 6 browser tabs pinned for competitor pricing pages. Monday morning ritual. Half the time I'd miss the Wednesday update anyway.

The 65-hour number matters for a different reason than you'd think. It's not the time cost — it's the inconsistency. Manual processes have gaps. Gaps mean you find out about a competitor's new free tier on a Thursday sales call, not when they actually changed it.

The alternative: automate the monitoring layer, review the AI digest. 10 minutes instead of 2 hours. Every change captured, not just the ones you happened to check on the right day.

Three things I track per competitor that I used to miss manually:
1. Changelog page (changes every ~3 days on average — the highest-signal surface)
2. Pricing page (42% change in any given month)
3. Features page (slower, but deprecations are invisible until you're in a deal)

The Monday ritual isn't a discipline problem. It's an architecture problem.
