The competitive intelligence market just split into two tiers and there's almost nothing in between.

**Tier 1: $20K–$40K/yr enterprise platforms**
Klue, Crayon (now SoftwareOne). Designed for teams with a dedicated CI analyst, Salesforce integration, quarterly business reviews. The full stack.

**Tier 2: Manual + free tools**
Google Alerts (catches news, misses website changes). Spreadsheets. A browser tab you bookmark and check when you remember.

There's almost nothing that automates the monitoring work for teams who don't have the budget or headcount for Tier 1.

Some context on what happened in 2025 that widened this gap:

— SoftwareOne acquired Crayon. Product roadmap now reports to an IT asset management conglomerate.

— Klue acquired Ignition and wound it down in 28 days. Then bolted their entire product onto Microsoft Teams, Dynamics 365, and Copilot MCP. If you're not on that stack, you're funding a roadmap for someone else.

— Adobe acquired Semrush. Kompyte's roadmap effectively froze pending integration decisions.

Every consolidation move pushes the enterprise tools further upmarket. The self-serve, affordable lane keeps getting less crowded.

I built KompWatch to live in the gap: automated website monitoring, AI summaries, $49/mo. No sales call. No 8-week implementation. Paste your competitor URLs and you're done.

We're early. But the market gap is getting clearer every quarter.
