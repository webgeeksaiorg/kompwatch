A prospect forwarded me a Crayon sales deck last year.

47 slides. They wanted to know if they could get something useful for tracking competitor pricing without committing to a $40,000/year contract.

That number isn't a guess. Vendr analyzed 106 Crayon contracts. Median: $40K/yr. That's before professional services, implementation, and the CI analyst you'll need to curate the daily intel feed — Crayon themselves estimate 5-10 hours/week of analyst time to run properly.

So the real cost is closer to $40K software + $30-50K of human time. Every year.

What that buys you: comprehensive monitoring of everything about your competitors. Press releases, job postings, G2 reviews, executive tweets, funding news, patent filings. Everything.

What most companies actually need: "tell me when competitor pricing pages change."

That's it. They don't need social listening or executive tracking or an analyst turning raw intel into a weekly newsletter that sales ignores.

We built KompWatch for that specific thing. $49/month. Paste URLs. Get an email when something changes, with an AI summary of what actually changed.

We do one thing. We don't have a 47-slide deck. 

If you're a PM, founder, or marketer doing CI part-time with a part-time budget — that's who this is for.
