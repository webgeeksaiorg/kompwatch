G2 reviewers rate Crayon's value-for-money at 3.0/5.

Average time to ROI: 14 months.

That means most customers churn before they ever get value. Not because the product is bad — because alert volume gets so high that processing it becomes a second full-time job. So people stop opening it.

The complaints are consistent: "too much noise, not enough signal." Crayon tracks everything. Social posts. Patent filings. Job listings. Press mentions. Blog updates. Every pricing page tweak. Every minor copy change.

That's not a bug. That's the product. But when everything is a signal, nothing is.

The average Crayon contract runs $20K–$100K/year. At that price, most companies assign someone to manage it full-time. So the real cost isn't the software — it's the software plus a headcount.

We built KompWatch to do one thing: watch competitor websites and tell you when something meaningful changes. You set a severity threshold. You get a weekly digest with the stuff that actually matters. No firehose. No dedicated CI team required.

$49/mo Pro tier. 10 competitors. Daily snapshots.

Not trying to replace Crayon for enterprise teams with dedicated competitive intelligence programs. Just trying to not require one.

If you're a PM, founder, or product team who keeps a spreadsheet of competitor URLs and checks them manually — this is for you.
