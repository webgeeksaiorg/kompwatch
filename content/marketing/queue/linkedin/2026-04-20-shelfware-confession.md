I talked to a PM last week who'd been paying for Crayon for 14 months.

She admitted she hadn't logged in since the third month.

$28,750/year. Still renewing. "It feels like admitting defeat to cancel."

There's a pattern in CI tools that nobody talks about: the onboarding is exciting. You configure your competitors, the alerts start flowing. Then the volume hits. 40-60 notifications a day. You start skimming. Then ignoring. Then the tab stays open but you stopped reading it weeks ago.

The problem isn't that teams don't care about competitor changes. They do. A lot.

The problem is signal-to-noise. $30K/year tools optimized for breadth — social mentions, job postings, review sites, press releases, patent filings — and somewhere in that avalanche is the one thing that actually matters: your competitor changed their pricing page on Tuesday.

That's the thing I'm building KompWatch to solve. Not comprehensive coverage. Just the stuff on their actual website, in plain English, when it changes.

You paste URLs. We watch them. When something changes, we tell you what changed and why it might matter.

$49/mo. No dedicated analyst required.

Still figuring out a lot. But "expensive RSS feed" is not the goal.
