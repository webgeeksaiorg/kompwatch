Shipped Cmd+K this week.

Hit it anywhere in KompWatch — search your competitors, jump to any digest, open settings. Fuzzy search, keyboard nav, the whole thing. Used cmdk, same library behind Vercel and Linear's palettes. Took about a day.

Two things I noticed immediately after shipping:

1. The product felt way more mature than it is. First impression changed noticeably.
2. Nobody asked for it. But the moment I used it I couldn't imagine the product without it.

I've been building KompWatch in public for a few months. Still pre-launch, still figuring out what resonates. The unsexy reality is that a lot of the work that makes a product feel good is exactly this kind of thing — features nobody's on the roadmap for, features that signal "someone who uses software built this."

Competitors like Crayon charge $500+/month partly because of product maturity perception. Some of that is real. Some of it is polish and trust signals.

Shipping Cmd+K isn't going to close deals. But it's the kind of thing that makes someone who lands on the product think "okay, this is serious." That's worth a day.

(If you're building SaaS and haven't used cmdk — 30 minutes, genuinely worth it.)
