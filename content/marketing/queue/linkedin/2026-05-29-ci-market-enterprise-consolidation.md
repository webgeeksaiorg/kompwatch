Two things happened in the competitive intelligence space this week that are worth paying attention to.

**Crayon launched an MCP server.**

The concept is sound: instead of logging into a dashboard to read competitor updates, your AI tools (Claude, Cursor, Copilot) can just pull the context directly. No tab-switching.

The catch: you need Crayon's enterprise tier to access it. That's roughly $50K/year. So the "AI-native CI" story is real, but it's gated behind an enterprise contract most product teams won't sign.

**Klue got named a Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader.**

Also good news for them. It means their positioning in enterprise sales conversations just got stronger. Procurement teams love a Gartner citation.

It also means Klue is firmly in the "we serve enterprise CI teams" category. The Gartner Magic Quadrant doesn't evaluate tools for a 12-person SaaS company that needs to know when their competitor changes their pricing page.

---

Here's what I think this actually means for the market:

The enterprise CI category is consolidating upward. Klue and Crayon are fighting for the same enterprise CIs budget. That fight requires analyst teams, implementation support, and multi-year contracts.

That creates a cleaner gap at the bottom. Not because we can do what they do — we can't. But because most teams don't need what they do.

Watch a few competitor pages. Get notified when something changes. Have an AI explain what changed and why it might matter.

That's a $49/mo problem, not a $50K/year problem.
