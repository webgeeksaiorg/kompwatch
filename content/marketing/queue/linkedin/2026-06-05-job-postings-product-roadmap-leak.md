Most product teams treat competitor job postings as noise.

They're actually one of the highest-signal inputs you have.

Here's why: companies don't hire reactively. By the time a job posting goes live, a decision was made 3-6 months ago. Budget was allocated. Scope was defined. You're seeing the output of a strategic commitment.

What to look for:

**"Staff ML Engineer, Recommendations" + "Senior PM, Personalization"**
They're building an AI layer on top of their core product. 12-18 month timeline.

**"Head of Enterprise Sales" + 3 SDRs**
Moving upmarket. Expect a new pricing tier or minimum seat requirement within 6 months.

**"Senior Integration Engineer" + "Partnerships Manager"**
Platform play incoming. They want to be in your workflow, not replace it.

**"Head of Customer Success" (when they didn't have one before)**
Churn problem. Probably means their product has gaps but their sales team is ahead of it.

I've been watching this for a year across a dozen SaaS competitors. The job posting signals predict product direction more reliably than reading their blog or watching their conference talks.

The problem: job listings are usually JavaScript-rendered. Most monitoring tools — and definitely Google Alerts — don't catch them.

Worth building into your competitive stack if you're not already.
