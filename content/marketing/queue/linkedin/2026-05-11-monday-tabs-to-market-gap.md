I built KompWatch because every Monday I had 6 browser tabs pinned to competitor pricing pages.

Not Klue. Not Crayon. Me, a spreadsheet, and the slow anxiety that I'd missed something while I was heads-down shipping.

That was the competitive intelligence process. For most of the teams I talked to, it still is.

**Here's where the market stands right now:**

Three of the four major CI platforms got absorbed into larger companies in the last 12 months.

SoftwareOne acquired Crayon. Klue cut 40% of staff and rebuilt their product on top of Microsoft/Teams. Adobe acquired Semrush last month — Kompyte came with it.

These are all still functional products. But they're roadmap line items inside companies whose primary businesses are something other than competitor monitoring.

**Klue's CEO publicly said they were "losing deals to ChatGPT."**

That's not a failure story. It's an honest signal about where enterprise CI value was actually sitting. A lot of it was "help me get background context on a competitor" — which LLMs now handle for free.

What LLMs don't do: tell you that your competitor's pricing page quietly added an Enterprise tier last Tuesday. That their /features page dropped two things they've been competing on. That they started running paid ads on your brand keywords three days ago.

That's a website monitoring problem, not an LLM problem.

**The gap we're building for:**

No dedicated CI analyst. No 8-week implementation. No $20K–$40K annual contract.

Just: paste your competitor URLs, set CSS selectors for the pages that matter, get an email when something changes. AI summarizes what actually changed in plain English.

$49/mo. We're early and we're still figuring out the marketing.

But the market gap is clearer now than when I started. Three of the major tools moved upmarket or got absorbed. The affordable, self-serve lane keeps getting less crowded.

If you're evaluating options after a Klue or Kompyte renewal: kompwatch.com/switch/klue and kompwatch.com/switch/kompyte — honest comparisons of who each product is actually built for.
