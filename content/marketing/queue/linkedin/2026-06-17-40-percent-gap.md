A study found that 40% of important competitive intelligence is never caught by Google Alerts.

Not because people set it up wrong. Because Google Alerts architecturally can't see what's on your competitors' product pages.

Every SaaS pricing page in 2026 is a JavaScript app. Content loads after the HTML. Google Alerts crawls the HTML. Which is an empty div until JS runs.

What gets missed:
— Pricing tier changes (the thing you most need to know about)
— Feature table updates (what moved to which plan)
— CTA copy changes (are they testing "Start free trial" vs "Contact sales"?)
— Trial flow restructuring

These don't appear in press releases. They don't get indexed by Google. They happen silently at 2 AM on a Tuesday and you find out 3 weeks later when someone mentions it in a board meeting.

I built KompWatch after spending 2 years manually checking 12 competitor websites every Monday morning and still missing things. The tool runs a real browser (headless Chromium via Playwright) on competitor pages every 6 hours and emails a plain-English summary of what changed.

$49/mo. Tracks what Google Alerts can't.

---

The question I keep asking product teams: when did you last actually look at your competitor's pricing page? Not recall it from memory — actually load it in a browser and look at it.

Most people who think they're on top of their competitive landscape are 2-4 weeks behind. Not because they're not paying attention. Because their tools are looking at the wrong thing.
