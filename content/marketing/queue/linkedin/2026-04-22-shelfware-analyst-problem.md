"We pay $28K a year for competitive intelligence. Half our reps don't know it exists."

Heard this from product managers at three different mid-market SaaS companies in the last month. Different industries, same situation.

The tools aren't broken. Crayon and Klue work. The problem is adoption — specifically that they were designed for a role most companies haven't hired.

There's a Cotera buyer guide quote that nails it: "By month three, the tool is a line item nobody questions because canceling feels like admitting defeat."

You don't cancel because the tool failed. You cancel because there was never a dedicated analyst to make it valuable.

I'm building for the team before that point. The team that doesn't have $28K and 15 analyst hours a week, but does want to know when a competitor changes their pricing page or suddenly posts five backend engineer job listings.

Narrower problem. Real problem.

If you're a PM or founder cobbling together Google Alerts + Visualping + manual tab-checking: that's the gap I'm building into. KompWatch is $49/month — paste URLs, set which sections to watch, get a morning digest.

Still early. But this is what I'm building and why.
