We're running an experiment this week: "Founding 100" pricing.

$397/year instead of $588 (annual Pro). Lock it in forever. First 100 only.

Here's the thinking — and the gamble.

**Why annual matters to us right now**

Pre-revenue, every signal of commitment is gold. Monthly churn is brutal on a product this early. An annual subscriber isn't just revenue — they're a signal that someone believes in the product's direction, not just today's feature set.

**Why it might not work**

$397 upfront is a harder ask than $49/month even if it's cheaper. People have to do math. They have to trust we'll still be here in 12 months. That's real friction.

Also: are the first 100 people who buy annual the ones who'll give the best feedback? Or do they self-select as "deal hunters" rather than "power users"? Still figuring this out.

**What we're measuring**

Conversion rate on the /pricing page this week vs. baseline. And qualitatively — who these people are, what they're tracking, what they actually care about.

The "Founding 100" framing is borrowed from indie makers who've done this well. It creates real urgency without being fake urgency. There are actually only 100 slots.

If it works: we've front-loaded 6 months of runway with committed users.  
If it doesn't: we learned something about price elasticity at this stage.

Building in public means sharing the bets before you know if they pay off.
