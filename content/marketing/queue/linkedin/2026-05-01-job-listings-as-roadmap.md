Job listings are a roadmap.

When a competitor posts for "Head of AI" — noise. Everyone's hiring AI people right now.

When they post for "Senior Engineer, Billing Infrastructure" — they're rebuilding how they handle payments. Reprice or new billing tier incoming.

When they post for "Pricing & Packaging Strategist" — they're about to reprice. Usually within 6 months. Every time.

When they post for "Enterprise Customer Success Manager x3" — they're moving upmarket. Watch for their homepage copy to shift from self-serve language to "book a demo."

I've tracked three competitors this way over two years. The job description changes came 3-6 months before every major pricing or positioning announcement. Without fail.

The reason this works: nobody writes a misleading job description. They pay for those results. The specificity in a job posting — the exact systems, the exact scope, the exact team it reports into — is an uncensored look at what the company is actually prioritizing.

Press releases are curated. Job listings aren't.

Probably the most underused free competitive intelligence source there is.
