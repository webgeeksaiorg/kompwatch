12 months. Three of the biggest CI platforms changed fundamentally.

Crayon: acquired for $1.4B by SoftwareOne — a 13,000-person IT procurement company. Competitive intelligence is now a product line inside a company whose main business is negotiating Microsoft licensing deals.

Klue: laid off 40% of their team. CEO cited "losing deals to ChatGPT." Pivoting toward AI workflow automation. Smaller team, different roadmap.

Kompyte: bundled into Semrush Enterprise. $499/mo minimum to access the CI features you were using standalone.

None of these companies are going away. But if you're a customer of any of them, your product is different than it was a year ago. The team that built it is smaller or it's owned by people whose core business isn't competitive intelligence.

Worth asking: is this tool still built for me?

I'm biased here — I run KompWatch, which is the $49/mo version of this. Paste competitor URLs, get plain-English alerts when something changes. That's the whole thing.

But even setting that aside: when your CI vendor just became a feature inside an IT procurement company, re-evaluation is probably healthy.

Simple has an underrated advantage right now. You know exactly what it does and who it's built for.
