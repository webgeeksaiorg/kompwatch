The competitive intelligence adoption problem is almost never about the quality of the intel.

It's about where the intel lands.

I've been looking at this a lot building KompWatch. The Salesforce internal CI team connected their competitive data to 3,000 CRM opportunities and 500+ reps via Slack. Result: 7x productivity improvement.

Not because the data got better. Because it showed up where decisions were already happening.

This is the actual pattern with Klue and Crayon too. Their own research shows less than 31% of reps access competitive content before a deal passes Stage 2. Same finding from Forrester. Enterprise tools with six-figure contracts and dedicated CI analysts — and 69% of reps still aren't using the battlecard.

The tool isn't the bottleneck. Distribution is.

If your CI workflow produces a PDF or a dashboard, you're still competing with inbox noise and context-switching. If it shows up in Slack when a competitor changes their pricing page, it becomes ambient intelligence. Your team knows without anyone having to go check.

This is why Slack and Teams delivery is the next thing I'm building into KompWatch. Not because it's technically impressive — it isn't. Because that's where the intel actually gets used.

Building in public. Working on it now.
