Every competitive intelligence vendor promises signal, not noise.

The 2026 data says nobody's actually solved it.

Crayon's #1 G2 complaint: "too much noise, not enough signal." 40-60 alerts per day across five competitors. 2-3 actionable per week. That's why they recommend a dedicated analyst 5-10 hours/week — the human becomes the filter.

New entrants are trying different approaches. Headsup.bot uses HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW priority classification with next-step recommendations. KompWatch (that's mine) uses a 0-100 confidence score per change. Pricing rewrite = 87. Footer link swap = 12. Set your own threshold.

Both approaches are an honest acknowledgment that raw monitoring output is useless at volume.

What I've noticed: the noise problem isn't about data quality. It's about what happens downstream. Every CI tool is optimized for "detecting the change." Almost none are optimized for "making the change useful to the person who needs to act on it."

That's the gap. Not better scraping — better last-mile delivery.

Still figuring this out. But at least we're naming the right problem now.
