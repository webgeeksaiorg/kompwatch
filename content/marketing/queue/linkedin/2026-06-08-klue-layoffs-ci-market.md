Klue just laid off 100 people. That's 40% of their staff.

I've been watching the enterprise CI market consolidate for a while, but this one hit different. Klue raised $62M to become the definitive competitive intelligence platform for enterprise GTM teams. They acquired Ignition last year to go further upmarket. And now 40% of the company is gone.

I'm not here to pile on. Building hard things is hard.

But here's what I keep thinking about:

The market they were chasing — enterprise companies with a dedicated competitive intelligence analyst, full PMM team, $30k+ software budget — is genuinely small. And it's getting smaller as AI changes how product teams work.

The underserved market is huge and everyone's ignoring it: startup founders and PMs who just need to know when a competitor changes their pricing page. No analyst. No 30-slide battlecard. Just "hey, they updated their pricing on Thursday."

I built KompWatch because I was manually checking 6 competitor websites every Monday morning. Paste URLs, get an email when something changes. That's it.

We're early and small. $49/mo. Maybe 100 miles from what Klue was doing.

But I think the CI market is bifurcating: enterprise platforms for massive GTM orgs, and lightweight monitoring tools for everyone else. The middle is getting hollowed out.

If you're a Klue customer figuring out your options — kompwatch.com/migrate/from-klue. No pressure, genuinely just trying to be useful.
