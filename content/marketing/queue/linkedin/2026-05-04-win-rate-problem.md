Crayon's State of Competitive Intelligence data: sales reps face a competitor in 68% of deals. Teams self-rate their competitive selling readiness at 3.8 out of 10.

The expensive fix is a CI platform + an analyst + weekly battlecard refreshes. Average cost: $25K–$100K/yr. Most teams either can't justify it or get shelfware by month 3.

The cheaper fix: watch the 5 pages that actually matter on every competitor's site — pricing, features, changelog, jobs, homepage hero. When something changes, you get a summary. Battlecard stays current. No analyst required.

The 3.8/10 problem isn't a training problem. It isn't a battlecard format problem. It's a "we found out about the competitor's new pricing tier after losing the deal" problem.

Monitoring isn't glamorous. But it's the thing that makes everything else — battlecards, enablement, objection handling — actually accurate.
