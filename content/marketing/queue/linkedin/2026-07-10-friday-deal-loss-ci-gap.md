We lost a deal last month because our battlecard had the wrong pricing.

Not wrong by a lot. The competitor had restructured their tiers — removed the entry tier, repackaged the middle one. Our AE was quoting against numbers that hadn't existed for 5 weeks.

The prospect knew. We didn't.

That's the thing about competitor changes that nobody talks about: they're not announced. No blog post. No press release. The page just quietly updates and the first time you find out is when a prospect brings it up in a call.

We've since automated monitoring on 8 pages per competitor. Pricing, features, homepage, changelog, enterprise, careers, docs, blog titles. We get a daily digest if anything changed.

Changed the prep conversation entirely. Now when a prospect mentions a competitor, we usually already know what changed and when.

The tool we use? I built it. KompWatch ($49/mo). Bit biased, obviously.

But the process works regardless of how you implement it. Automate the detection, reserve human time for the analysis. Don't use a Notion doc that one person updates quarterly.

What are the pages on your competitor list you wish you'd been watching earlier?
