I spent 3 years manually checking competitor websites every Monday morning.

6 browser tabs. Pricing pages, features pages. I had a ritual: coffee, open tabs, scan for anything new.

Half the time I'd miss a Wednesday change. The other half, the "change" turned out to be a rotating testimonial or a footer copyright year update.

Then I missed a real one. A competitor dropped their starter price by 40% on a Thursday. I found out from a lost sales call — the customer asked why they were so much cheaper. I had no answer.

That weekend I wrote a cron job. HTML diff emailed to myself. Embarrassingly simple.

That dumb script caught three more pricing changes in the next two months. It paid for itself immediately.

The cron job grew. I added AI to translate the raw HTML diffs into plain English. Added screenshots. Added a proper UI so my team could see it too.

Now it's CompeteWatch. $49/mo. Tracks competitor websites, tells you in plain English when something changes.

No enterprise contract. No 30-slide pitch about "competitive intelligence strategy." You paste URLs, we watch them, you get an email when something changes.

If you're still doing Monday tab-checking: you don't have to.
