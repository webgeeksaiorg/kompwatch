There's a 3-hour window that matters in competitive deals.

When a competitor changes their pricing, the teams that know within a few hours can brief the AE, update the talk track, adjust the next proposal. The teams that find out 3 weeks later — from a prospect on a call — are playing catch-up.

Same information. Completely different outcome based on when it arrived.

This is what I kept thinking about when building KompWatch. The monitoring part is table stakes. Any tool can tell you "something changed on a competitor's website." The real question is how fast, and does the alert actually tell you what changed (before and after) so someone can act on it without spending 20 minutes hunting around the page?

Most of the competitor monitoring tools I tried before building my own sent me "change detected" emails that I had to manually investigate. Which meant I didn't. Which meant the alert was useless.

The thing that works: a notification that says "Enterprise tier: was $199/seat → now $249/seat. Effective immediately." Takes two seconds to read. The AE can act on it in the next call.

The speed of knowing matters. So does the specificity of the signal. Both have to be right.
