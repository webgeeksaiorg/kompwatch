Adobe closed the Semrush acquisition yesterday. $1.9 billion.

The pitch is "complete digital intelligence" for enterprise marketing teams. I get it — Adobe sells to CMOs at Fortune 500 companies who want one vendor for everything.

That's not the customer I'm building for.

Here's what that $1.9B deal signals: the tools that tell you what competitors are actually doing keep moving upmarket. Semrush's cheapest plan is $100+/mo just for SEO. The CI platforms (Crayon, Klue) run $20K–$100K/year. All of them assume you have a dedicated analyst to run them. Most SaaS teams don't.

The PM at a 20-person company who needs to know when their main competitor changed their pricing page or launched a new feature — that person is still cobbling together Google Alerts and a Monday morning tab ritual.

That's the gap I'm building into.

KompWatch watches competitor websites, tells you what changed, summarizes it in plain English. $49/mo. No contract. No analyst. No 30-slide pitch deck about "competitive intelligence strategy."

The consolidation at the enterprise end actually makes the problem worse for everyone else. As the platforms get more integrated and more expensive, the mid-market gets left further behind.

Every time a company spends $2B buying into the enterprise CI space, there's more room at the bottom for something that just does the one thing.
