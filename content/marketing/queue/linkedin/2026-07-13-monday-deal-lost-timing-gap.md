A deal we lost taught me more about competitor monitoring than any strategy article.

We lost it because our competitor had dropped their enterprise tier price 6 weeks before the call. We didn't know. Our pricing objection handling was based on the *old* number. The prospect knew the new one.

That's not a strategy problem. It's a signal problem.

We had Google Alerts set up. We weren't ignoring competitive intel. We just had a tool that couldn't actually detect that change — because their pricing page is a React SPA, and Google Alerts doesn't render JavaScript.

After that we built our own monitoring. Headless browser, specific pages watched, plain-English AI summary of what changed. Set it up in an afternoon. 

Three months later KompWatch is a product other teams use.

Here's the thing I keep coming back to: the teams that lost deals to competitor moves weren't lazy or naive. They had the Google Alerts. They checked competitor sites sometimes. They just didn't have something that caught the signal at the right time.

That's the whole gap. Not a strategy gap. A timing gap.
