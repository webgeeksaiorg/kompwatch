Google Alerts does one job well.

That job is not watching your competitor's pricing page.

It monitors for indexed content — news, press releases, new blog posts. Useful. But your competitor isn't going to write a press release when they quietly raise prices 20% or kill their startup tier. That change just happens. The number goes up. The plan disappears.

When I was manually tracking competitors, I had a tab folder called "Monday CI" with 12 tabs. Prices, features, homepage. Opened them every Monday, scanned for changes, closed them. About 40 minutes.

I missed stuff constantly. Things that changed on a Wednesday. Things that changed while I was on vacation. Things that changed on a page I had mis-bookmarked.

The problem isn't effort. The problem is that manual checks are periodic and websites change continuously.

Google Alerts doesn't fix that. It adds a second periodic signal that covers different content than the thing you actually need to watch.

What actually helps: something that loads the page (headless browser, because pricing pages are usually React apps), diffs what changed, and tells you in English. Not a raw HTML diff — an English sentence.

"Competitor added Enterprise tier at $199/mo. Previous top tier was $99/mo."

That's the CI that matters in a deal.

I built KompWatch because I got tired of waiting for Monday to find out what changed on Tuesday.
