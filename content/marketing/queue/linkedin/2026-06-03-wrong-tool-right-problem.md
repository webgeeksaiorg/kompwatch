The #1 misconception about competitive intelligence tools: they're all doing the same thing.

They're not.

Crayon and Klue are sales enablement platforms. Their core value prop is battlecards — curated competitive content that helps sales reps handle objections in deals. That requires a dedicated product marketer or CI analyst to configure the alerts, filter the noise, and synthesize the output into usable format.

For a 50-person company with a GTM team, that's a reasonable investment. You're paying $20-40K/yr, but you're enabling 15 reps to close more deals.

For a 12-person startup with no GTM team, you're paying $20-40K/yr for alerts nobody has time to read.

The "right" tool depends on what question you're actually trying to answer.

If the question is "what should my battlecard say about Competitor X" — you want Klue.

If the question is "did any of my three main competitors change their pricing or feature set this week" — you want something that watches those specific pages and emails you a summary. That's a $49/mo problem, not a $20K one.

We built KompWatch for the second question. No battlecard infrastructure. No CI analyst required. Paste URLs, set severity thresholds, get a weekly digest.

Not trying to be Crayon. Just trying to answer a different question.
