Adobe just acquired Semrush for $1.9B. If you use Kompyte, you should be paying attention.

Kompyte was acquired by Semrush in 2022. Semrush is now being absorbed into Adobe's enterprise marketing stack. That's two acquisitions in three years for a tool that was originally sold as "simple competitive monitoring for your sales team."

Here's the pattern I've seen play out: When a specific-purpose tool gets acquired into a broader platform, the product roadmap shifts toward the acquirer's existing customer base. Pricing usually goes up. Support teams merge. The feature that was the main reason you signed up stops being the priority.

I'm not saying Kompyte will get worse — maybe Adobe invests in it heavily. But the 6-12 months post-acquisition are always murky for customers who care about a specific use case.

Klue's in a similar spot — they acquired Ignition (AI/agentic tools) in September 2025, the entire Ignition team left to found DoubleO AI, and Klue has been moving deeper into enterprise sales enablement and away from the core website monitoring use case.

Crayon built an MCP server to integrate with enterprise AI platforms (Copilot, Glean). Great tech. Still $30K+/year, still assumes a dedicated analyst.

The SMB lane — "I need to know when a competitor changes their pricing page, I can spend $49/month, I don't need a dedicated analyst" — is getting wider, not narrower. Every acquisition and enterprise pivot clears more space.

That's the market I'm building KompWatch for.
