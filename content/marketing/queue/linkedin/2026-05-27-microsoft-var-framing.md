SoftwareOne reported Q1 2026 earnings this week. Revenue jumped 67.4%.

The driver wasn't competitive intelligence. It was Microsoft cloud agreements and licensing volume from the Crayon customer base.

SoftwareOne is a Microsoft VAR. A very large, very competent one. That's their core business. Software licensing. Cloud reselling. The Crayon acquisition gave them a distribution channel into PMM and sales enablement teams — customers who buy software and who might also need Microsoft cloud infrastructure.

Crayon's CI product is not why SoftwareOne bought Crayon. They bought the customer relationships.

None of this makes Crayon a bad product. The team is still there (for now). The features still work. But the product roadmap is now shaped by what moves enterprise software licensing margins — not what helps a 15-person SaaS startup know when a competitor changes their pricing page.

This is the same story playing out at Kompyte (inside Adobe, three acquisitions deep) and Klue (just raised $62M and bought 4 companies in 5 months, roadmap pointing at enterprise sales enablement, not monitoring).

I built KompWatch because none of these tools were ever going to solve my problem. I'm a single founder who needed to stop checking 12 browser tabs every Monday morning. The tools that existed were either enterprise contracts I couldn't afford or free tools that broke on React sites.

The CI market keeps consolidating upward. The gap at the bottom keeps growing.

Not complaining about it. Just noting it — because if you're a small SaaS team trying to figure out what to use right now, the honest answer is: the tool you're thinking about probably isn't being built for you anymore.
