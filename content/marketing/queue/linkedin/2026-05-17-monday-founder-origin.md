For 18 months I had the same Monday ritual.

Open six browser tabs. Check each competitor's pricing page. Check the features page. Look for anything that changed since last week.

Half the time I'd forget. The other half I'd miss something because it changed on a Wednesday, not a Monday.

The thing is — pricing changes almost never get announced. A SaaS company quietly bumps $99 → $129? No email. No changelog entry. No LinkedIn post. The number just changes, and if you're not watching, you miss it.

That specific pain is what made me build KompWatch. Not because I wanted to build a "competitive intelligence platform." Just because I wanted to stop tabbing through browser windows every Monday morning.

The AI layer was actually the second thing I built, not the first. First version was literally just "did the HTML of this page change significantly?" That was enough to be useful. The summaries came later, when I realized raw diffs were unreadable without context.

Still figuring out what the right scope is. We don't do social listening. We don't track patents. We watch websites. That's it.

For most small teams, that's enough.
