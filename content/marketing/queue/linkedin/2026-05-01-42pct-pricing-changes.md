42% of SaaS companies adjusted their pricing in the first half of 2024.

None of them sent a press release.

They changed the page on a Tuesday. Sometimes they reworked the tier names. Sometimes they buried a per-seat clause in the fine print. Sometimes they just removed the annual discount — quietly, no announcement.

Here's the problem: most teams find out about a competitor pricing change when a customer asks about it during a renewal conversation. That's the worst possible time.

The gap isn't awareness — everyone knows they should track competitor pricing. The gap is the actual mechanism. Manual spot-checks miss 60% of changes that happen mid-week. Google Alerts doesn't monitor your competitor's pricing page; it monitors who's writing *about* your competitor. And the enterprise CI tools (Crayon, Klue) are built for teams with a dedicated analyst — not the PM who has 30 other things to do.

The $49/month answer: set a headless browser on the specific URLs that matter (pricing page, features page, homepage), get an AI digest when something actually changes, move on.

Pricing pages change constantly and quietly. The teams that know first have the best chance to respond.
