I used to spend Monday mornings with 6 browser tabs pinned.

Competitor pricing pages. Feature pages. Changelog pages. 45 minutes of clicking around. Half the time nothing changed. The other half, something had changed on Wednesday — 5 days ago — and I found out about it today.

The problem wasn't the time. It was the asymmetry. My competitors don't change on my schedule. They ship features on Tuesdays. They update pricing on Wednesdays. They quietly add a new tier on a Thursday night.

A Monday check misses all of that.

So I built a cron job. Then I added diffs. Then I added AI summaries so I actually understood what changed instead of staring at HTML.

That became KompWatch.

Not because I wanted to compete with Crayon or Klue. I genuinely couldn't afford them. $25K/year for a 4-person startup is not a line item that gets approved.

We're at $49/mo Pro. Watch up to 10 competitors. Get alerts in plain English.

The Monday tab ritual is optional now. I still do it sometimes. Old habits.

But when I do it, I already know what changed.
