Gartner says 40% of tech companies will use competitive intelligence tools by 2026. Up from 10% a few years ago.

I've been thinking about what that actually means.

The jump is real. Competitive intel went from "nice to have" to standard practice for product and GTM teams. But here's what the stat doesn't say:

Most of those tools cost $20K–$40K per year. Crayon median is $28,750/year (Vendr data, 90+ purchases). Klue is $30,000. Both require a dedicated person to extract value from them.

So what happens when the 22-person SaaS company decides to "use CI tools"? They get a sales call. They see the quote. They go back to manually checking 8 browser tabs every Monday morning.

That's the gap I kept falling into.

I built KompWatch because I was that 22-person company. Six pinned tabs. Half the time I'd forget to check. The other half I'd miss a change that happened on a Wednesday — three days before a sales call where the competitor had already updated their pricing.

$49/mo. Paste URLs. Get a Monday email with what changed, in plain English.

Not trying to be Crayon. Just trying to be the thing that actually gets used.
