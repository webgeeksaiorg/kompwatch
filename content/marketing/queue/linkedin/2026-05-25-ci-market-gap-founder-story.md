Three acquisitions. One gap.

Semrush → Adobe ($1.9B, April 2026). Kompyte → Crayon → SoftwareOne. The affordable middle of the competitive intelligence market is gone.

What's left: Google Alerts (free, useless for SaaS) or $20K/year enterprise contracts built for 500-person companies with a dedicated PMM.

The product I wish existed when I was running a 12-person team:
- Monitors competitor websites automatically (not just blog RSS)
- Catches pricing page changes, feature additions, positioning shifts
- Summarizes what changed in plain English, not a 40-page report
- Costs $49/mo, no annual contract, no onboarding call

So I built it. KompWatch has been running for 4 months. 85+ pieces of content in the queue (marketing is weirdly the hardest part of building in public). Still pre-launch, but the product actually works.

The competitive intelligence market needed a solo-founder product. This is mine.

Founders: if you're still manually checking competitor websites on Monday mornings — you're not alone. That was exactly me 18 months ago.
