The hardest part of tracking competitor websites isn't building the monitor. It's knowing what you missed before you built it.

Last year I found out a competitor had removed their integration with Salesforce — quietly, no announcement — by accident. A prospect mentioned it in a call. "We heard [competitor] broke their Salesforce sync. Is yours stable?"

That integration change had been gone for six weeks. Six weeks of that being a potential win in every enterprise deal. We had no idea.

That's when I started building KompWatch.

It's not a Crayon or Klue replacement. I'm not trying to build a $20M enterprise CI platform. It does one specific thing: watches the pages you tell it to watch and tells you in plain English what changed. Pricing, features, integrations, CTAs — whatever's on the page.

$49/mo. No sales call. No implementation consultant.

The first week I had it running, I caught a competitor adding "Now SOC 2 Certified" to their security page. We were in a deal where the prospect had asked about security posture that morning.

That's the whole pitch, honestly.
