Three major acquisitions in the competitive intelligence space in under 18 months:

— Adobe acquired Semrush (which had already acquired Kompyte)
— G2 acquired Gartner Digital Markets (Capterra, Software Advice, GetApp)
— Klue raised another big round and repositioned toward enterprise

What this means if you're a startup or mid-size SaaS team:

The $500-2K/month "mid-market" tier is quietly disappearing. The tools that used to serve 20-person product teams are being repositioned for enterprise buyers, with pricing to match.

It's not malicious — it's just how acquisition math works. The acquiring company needs revenue growth, and enterprise seats are worth more than startup seats.

I started building KompWatch because I needed a tool in that middle range and couldn't justify $15K/year for something where I only needed 30% of the features. 

Still building it for that same team.

The consolidation is genuinely frustrating if you've been a customer of one of these platforms for years. And it's probably an opportunity if you're evaluating new tools right now — before prices adjust further.
