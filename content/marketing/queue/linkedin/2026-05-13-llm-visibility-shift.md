The competitive intelligence problem just got a second layer.

Before: monitor competitor websites for pricing, feature, and changelog changes.

Now: also monitor where competitors appear in ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Claude results.

Gartner is projecting a 25% drop in traditional search traffic by end of 2026. Buyers are querying AI before they ever visit a website. That means a prospect might never Google your competitor — they'll just ask an AI, get an answer, and shortlist based on that.

Two startups (Peec AI, AIclicks) already launched specifically to track LLM visibility. Neither existed 12 months ago. That's how fast this is moving.

The traditional monitoring layer — pricing pages, feature announcements, changelogs — is table stakes now. The new question is: when a buyer asks an AI who the best options are, do you show up?

Working on adding LLM visibility tracking to KompWatch. The surface area of competitive intelligence is expanding faster than most teams realize.
