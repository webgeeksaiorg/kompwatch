Two of the three biggest names in competitive intelligence had a rough 12 months. Here's what actually happened and what it means if you're buying.

**Crayon** was acquired by SoftwareOne in July 2025 for ~$1.4B. SoftwareOne is a Swiss IT and cloud licensing company — CHF 1.6B revenue, 13,000 employees, 70+ countries. They publicly targeted CHF 80–100M in annual cost synergies within 18 months of closing.

**Klue** cut 40% of staff (~85 people) in June 2025, framed as an AI reboot. Three months later, acquired Ignition — IP-only, no employees. Ignition customers got a 30-day wind-down window.

I'm not predicting doom for either product. Both have real customer bases and real revenue. But "cost synergy target within 18 months" and "40% staff cut to hit profitability" are signals worth weighing if you're the one signing the renewal.

Things I'd do before signing either:

1. Read the change-of-control clause in your current contract (or make sure it's in the new one)
2. Note your renewal date. If it's in the next 6 months, have a comparison shortlist ready — even if you plan to renew. It's leverage.
3. Ask your AE directly: what's the product roadmap for the next 12 months post-acquisition? Get it in writing if you can.

The competitive intelligence market isn't going away. Neither product is collapsing. But the era of "sign a 3-year Crayon deal and not think about it" is probably over.
