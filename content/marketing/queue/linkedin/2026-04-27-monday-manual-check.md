For two years, Monday morning started the same way.

Open six browser tabs. Competitor pricing pages. Their features lists. One of them had a changelog I checked manually. Another didn't — I'd scan the homepage and try to remember what it looked like the week before.

Sometimes I'd catch something useful. A new tier added at $99/month that undercut us on the SMB side. A feature we'd been planning listed as "coming soon" on their roadmap. Once, I noticed a competitor had quietly removed their annual discount — sign of pricing confidence, or cash flow trouble, I never figured out which.

Mostly I didn't catch things. Found out weeks later when a customer mentioned it. Or from a sales rep who lost a deal and only then checked the competitor's page.

The problem wasn't effort. I was putting in the effort. The problem was that I was doing surveillance with my eyeballs, manually, once a week. Any change that happened on a Tuesday was invisible until the following Monday.

Built the automated version of that Monday ritual. Headless browser, checks daily (or hourly on Pro), AI summarizes the diffs into plain English, emails it.

Not a competitive intelligence platform. Doesn't do win/loss, battlecards, or social listening. Just does the Monday check automatically, seven days a week.

$49/month. No dedicated analyst required. No 30-day onboarding.

If you're still doing the tab-by-tab manual check — been there. There's a better way now.
