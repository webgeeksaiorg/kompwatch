There's a cost nobody includes in the Crayon or Klue ROI calculator.

The platform doesn't run itself.

Someone has to read the alerts, filter the noise, decide what's signal, write the battle cards, update them when a competitor changes pricing, and make sure sales actually uses them.

That's a part-time job at minimum. At many enterprise companies it's a full-time headcount. "Competitive Intelligence Manager" is a real role — median salary around $95K.

So the math looks like this:
- Crayon: ~$40K/yr (Vendr median, 106 deals)
- Klue: $15-20K/yr minimum
- CI Manager salary: $80-120K/yr

You're not buying a monitoring tool. You're standing up a small department.

For big companies with 50+ reps and multiple product lines: probably worth it. For a 10-person startup trying to stay on top of 3 competitors? Genuinely not.

That's why I built KompWatch as a $49/mo tool that just does the specific thing most teams actually need: tells you when a competitor's website changes and explains what changed in plain English.

No battle card system. No CI analyst workflow. No Slack-to-Salesforce integration.

Just: something changed here's what and here's why it might matter.

Some teams need Crayon. Most don't.
