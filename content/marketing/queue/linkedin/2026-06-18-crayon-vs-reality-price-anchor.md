I used to think Crayon was expensive.

Then I actually looked at the number.

$25,000–$50,000 per year. No self-serve signup. No monthly option. You talk to a sales rep, you get a contract, you commit 12 months minimum.

For a lot of teams — that's not a purchase decision. That's a budget cycle.

I know this because I built KompWatch after I couldn't justify that number to myself. I was a founding PM, tracking 6 competitors manually every Monday. Six browser tabs. Trying to remember what the pricing page looked like last week.

What I actually needed wasn't a $25K intelligence platform. I needed something that would email me when a competitor's pricing page changed.

That's $49/mo. No contract. No sales call. Credit card, and you're set up in 5 minutes.

---

The framing that gets me: Crayon (now under SoftwareOne) is still priced for teams that have a dedicated competitive intelligence analyst. That's maybe 15% of SaaS companies.

The other 85% have a PM who opens a spreadsheet on Monday and tries to remember what changed.

KompWatch is for that 85%. It watches websites, tells you what changed, skips the noise.

No demo required. No enterprise contract. No 7-week onboarding.
