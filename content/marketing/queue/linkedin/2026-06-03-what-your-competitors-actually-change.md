After a year of automated competitor monitoring, here's what I've learned actually changes — and what doesn't.

**Changes constantly (mostly noise):**
- Testimonials and social proof
- Blog content (they're publishing, good for them)
- Minor copy tweaks

**Changes rarely but matters when it does:**
- Pricing structure (not just numbers — new tiers, removed discounts, bundled seats)
- Feature positioning (what they're leading with, what they're burying)
- Navigation (what's top-level vs dropdown)

**Almost never changes but is gold when it does:**
- Job listings referenced on product pages
- Integration logos (Salesforce appearing = going upmarket)
- Case study verticals (who they're targeting now vs 12 months ago)

The insight that surprised me: competitors change their navigation more than their pricing.

Moving something from a footer dropdown to a top-level nav item means they figured out that feature converts. That's a stronger signal than a $10 price change.

None of that shows up in a Google Alert. Barely shows up in a changelog. You catch it by watching the page.

42% of SaaS pricing pages change at least once per month. Monthly manual audits miss four in ten of those shifts.

That's why I built KompWatch — not because checking competitors manually is hard, but because it's reliably incomplete.
