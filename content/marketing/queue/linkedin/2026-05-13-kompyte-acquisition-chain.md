If you're using Kompyte for competitor monitoring, here's the ownership chain:

Kompyte → acquired by Semrush → Semrush acquired by Adobe (February 2026).

That's three layers. Your "affordable SMB competitor monitoring tool" is now a line item inside one of the largest enterprise software companies in the world.

Nobody's killing Kompyte tomorrow. But product priorities follow organizational incentives, and Adobe's incentives are pointed at enterprise creative and marketing suites — not helping a 15-person SaaS team track what Competitor X did to their pricing page last Wednesday.

I've seen this pattern before with Pardot (Salesforce), Marketo (Adobe again, actually), and ExactTarget. The tool doesn't disappear. It just stops getting the roadmap investment that made it useful. Features that would serve SMBs get deprioritized. Integrations you need don't get built. The price doesn't drop.

This isn't a criticism of Kompyte specifically. The people who built it built something genuinely useful. It's just the reality of acquisition math.

If you're evaluating CI tools right now, "who owns this and what are their incentives" is worth asking alongside "does it do what I need."
