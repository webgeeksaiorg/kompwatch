Interesting framing I came across this week: competitive intelligence has two phases that most tools only solve one of.

**Discovery**: Who are my competitors? What are they building? Already.dev does this — scans Product Hunt, GitHub, Reddit, Hacker News, app stores, patent databases. $49/month.

**Monitoring**: What are my known competitors changing right now? That's KompWatch. Watch the pricing page, the features list, the job board. Real headless browser, so it handles SPAs. AI digest every morning in plain English. $49/month.

Most teams conflate these two problems and end up with a tool that doesn't quite solve either.

The complete stack is under $100/month. No Crayon contract. No seven-week onboarding. No dedicated analyst required.

Still figuring out the right way to talk about this. But the "discovery vs monitoring" framing feels like the clearest breakdown I've found.
