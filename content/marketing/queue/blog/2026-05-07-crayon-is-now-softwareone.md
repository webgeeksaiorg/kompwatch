# Crayon Is Now SoftwareOne. Here's What That Actually Means.

Crayon finished its rebrand in January 2026. If you search for Crayon's website now, you land on SoftwareOne's product pages. The Crayon brand is gone.

This isn't a rumor or a PR exercise. SoftwareOne completed the acquisition in mid-2025 and spent the second half of the year migrating customers and sunsetting the standalone Crayon identity. As of Q1 2026, it's SoftwareOne Competitive Intelligence.

If you're evaluating CI tools right now — or you're on a Crayon renewal — here's an honest read of what changed, what didn't, and what it means for your budget.

## What SoftwareOne Actually Is

SoftwareOne is a software procurement and licensing management company. Their core business: helping large enterprises buy Microsoft, SAP, and other enterprise software licenses at scale. They're publicly traded in Switzerland. Revenue ~$1B/year.

Competitive intelligence is not their core business. It's an acquisition.

That's not a criticism — Microsoft acquires companies that aren't core to their business all the time and runs them well. But the context matters. When you're a customer of a product that's been acquired by a company whose primary market is IT procurement for Fortune 500 companies, you're now a line item in a different strategic conversation.

## What Changed (So Far)

**The product is mostly intact.** Core Crayon features — website monitoring, battlecard generation, news/social tracking, CRM integrations — appear to be maintained. Customer support infrastructure transferred.

**Pricing opacity increased.** Crayon was never transparent on pricing ($20-40K/year was the public range, negotiable down in some cases), but at least you could find reference points in review sites and sales conversations. Under SoftwareOne, pricing is now wrapped into procurement discussions. Multiple buyers report being quoted alongside other SoftwareOne product bundles. Apples-to-apples comparison is harder.

**Product roadmap is unclear.** SoftwareOne has not published a public product roadmap for their competitive intelligence product. What gets built next, and for whom, is not visible.

**SMB focus is lower.** SoftwareOne's existing customer base is enterprise. The go-to-market motion — where leads go, how they're nurtured, who gets attention from the sales and CS teams — has shifted toward larger accounts.

## What Didn't Change

The underlying problem Crayon was solving still exists. Product and marketing teams need to know when competitors change things. Battlecards need to stay current. Sales teams need competitive context delivered in their workflow.

That problem didn't get acquired. It just doesn't have a standalone Crayon to address it anymore.

## Who This Matters To

**You're on an existing contract:** Probably fine until renewal. Nothing has changed in the product that forces immediate action. Start asking about the roadmap and pricing structure for your renewal cycle 90 days early.

**You're evaluating Crayon for the first time:** You're not evaluating Crayon. You're evaluating SoftwareOne's CI product. Ask specifically: what is the product roadmap for the next 12 months, what team is building it, and what percentage of SoftwareOne's engineering investment goes into competitive intelligence vs. their core licensing business.

**You're under 100 employees:** Enterprise procurement companies are not optimized for you. The sales process, the pricing, the support tier — none of it is built for a 30-person SaaS team who needs to watch 8 competitor websites. You were probably at the edge of Crayon's ICP before the acquisition. Post-acquisition, you're further from center.

**You're on the SMB-to-mid-market path:** Worth looking at what's available now. The CI market has more options in the $50-500/month range than it did two years ago, specifically because the enterprise platforms have moved upmarket and left space.

## The Actual Decision Framework

Before you renew or switch, answer three questions:

**1. Do you have someone whose job is competitive intelligence?**
Not "someone who does CI on the side." A dedicated PMM, CI analyst, or product strategist who owns this. Enterprise CI platforms need this person. Without them, you get the tool but not the outcomes.

If yes: SoftwareOne's product may still be the right call. It's an established platform with serious features.

If no: you're probably buying more platform than you need.

**2. What specifically do you need monitored?**
If your answer is "competitor websites — pricing, features, changelog" — that's a dedicated monitoring problem. You don't need battlecard management infrastructure. You need automated alerts with AI summaries when a page changes.

That problem costs $50-150/month to solve in 2026, not $25K/year. Tools like [KompWatch](/pricing) do exactly this.

If your answer is "battlecards, sales enablement, win/loss analysis, everything" — then you need a platform with more surface area. SoftwareOne, Klue, or a custom stack.

**3. How much of your current Crayon contract are you actually using?**
Honest answer from renewal data: most teams use 20-30% of what enterprise CI platforms offer. If you're primarily using website monitoring and email digests, you're carrying 70% unused product at enterprise pricing.

## The Bottom Line

Crayon as a standalone product no longer exists. SoftwareOne's competitive intelligence offering is a rebranded version maintained by a company whose core focus is elsewhere.

That's not a reason to panic if you're an existing customer. It is a reason to evaluate your options with clear eyes when renewal comes up.

## FAQ

**Is Crayon shutting down?**
No. The product continues under SoftwareOne's umbrella. Existing contracts are being honored. The Crayon brand name is gone, but the product is operational.

**Does Crayon still have a free trial?**
Crayon never had a public free trial — they've always been demo-first. Under SoftwareOne this remains true.

**What's the difference between Crayon and Klue now?**
Both target mid-market to enterprise, both in the $20-40K/year range. Klue remains independent. Crayon is now SoftwareOne. Klue has been more active on product (acquiring Ignition in 2025, releasing Compete Agent) — for roadmap momentum, Klue currently has clearer forward motion.

**Why did SoftwareOne acquire Crayon?**
Strategic expansion into software intelligence. SoftwareOne's core product is software asset management — knowing what software an enterprise is running and what it costs. Competitive intelligence is an adjacent capability that extends their value proposition to procurement and vendor management teams.

**What's the cheapest alternative to Crayon for small SaaS teams?**
Depends on what you're trying to do. For pure website monitoring (when competitor pages change): $0 with a DIY Playwright script, or $49/month with KompWatch for managed monitoring. For full battlecard management: no cheap equivalent exists — that's a content-and-process problem, not just a tool problem.
