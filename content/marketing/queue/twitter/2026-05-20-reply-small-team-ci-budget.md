The economics are weird at small scale.

Crayon starts around $15K/year. Klue is $10-20K. For a 5-person startup that's real money and you probably need 1-2 people to actually use it.

The alternative most people land on: one PM checks 6 competitor tabs every Monday. Works until it doesn't. You miss a pricing change that costs you a deal and suddenly it feels expensive to not have monitoring.

The middle ground exists. Just smaller tools, not enterprise contracts.
