Klue laid off 40% of their team last year. Then acquired Ignition (agentic AI for product marketers) three months later — IP only. No employees came over. Ignition customers got a discount offer and a 30-day wind-down notice.

Their CEO said out loud that the cuts were driven by uncertainty over "what companies are willing to pay for versus building themselves in the age of AI."

That's a genuinely interesting thing to say publicly. They're not wrong that the question exists. But it's a weird thing to telegraph to enterprise customers mid-renewal cycle.
