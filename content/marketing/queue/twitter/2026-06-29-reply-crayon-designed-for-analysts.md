The thing about Crayon and Klue is they were designed for teams that have a dedicated competitive intelligence analyst. Someone whose full-time job is to turn raw data into battle cards.

Most companies below $50M ARR don't have that person. So the tool sits there generating data nobody processes.

Not a knock on Crayon. They built exactly what their market wants. The gap is for companies where "CI" is just the PM doing it 3 hours a week.
