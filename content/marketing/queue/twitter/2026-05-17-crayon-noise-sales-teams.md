The most common reason sales teams stop using Crayon isn't the price.

It's the volume.

You set it up. 800 alerts in the first week. Your inbox becomes a digest of competitor blog posts, press releases, podcast mentions, and job listing updates. All equal priority. All requiring you to decide if it matters.

Eventually people just stop opening the emails.

Good data buried in noise is the same as no data. Signal scoring isn't a nice-to-have. It's whether the tool gets used at all.
