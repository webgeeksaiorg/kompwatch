90 days after buying Crayon or Klue, most teams have stopped reading the alerts.

The tool keeps running. The inbox keeps filling. Nobody's looking.

It turns into a $2,500/month line item that feels too embarrassing to cancel because someone on the leadership team bought it with conviction.

I've heard this story from 6 different product managers this year. "We have Crayon, we just don't really use it."

That's not a Crayon problem specifically. It's what happens when a tool requires a dedicated owner and you don't have one. It becomes an expensive RSS feed.

The thing I'm trying to build is the opposite: works for a team of 3, no babysitting required. Digests, not dashboards.
