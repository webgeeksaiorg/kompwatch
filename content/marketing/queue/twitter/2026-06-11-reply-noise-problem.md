The noise problem is real and I've broken my own tool with it.

Early version of KompWatch fired on anything — A/B tests rotating, cookie banners shifting, tracking pixels loading. Users ignored everything within two weeks.

What worked: monitoring text content and structured elements specifically, ignoring scripts and style changes. And only alerting when the AI summary can actually say something specific happened.

Still not perfect. But "99% silence, rare useful alert" beats "constant noise, useful alerts buried."
