Klue just got named a Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader.

Here's what that means in practice: they'll show that badge in every sales deck. Your procurement team will see it. The deal will feel "safe."

And then you'll pay $20K/year, spend 10 weeks onboarding, and realize you needed someone to run the thing 8 hours a week to make it useful.

Gartner doesn't measure whether teams actually use the tool. They measure features, vision, and execution. Not "did the $2M company actually get ROI."

If you have a CI analyst, Klue is probably fine. If you don't, you just bought an expensive dashboard nobody opens.
