Google Alerts catches mentions. It doesn't catch changes.

Things it will miss:
- Competitor quietly removes their free tier (page edit, no announcement)
- Pricing restructure from "contact sales" to published prices
- Feature page rewritten to go after your ICP
- New use-case landing page targeting your segment
- Four ML engineer job postings that telegraph the next product push

The indexing lag also matters. Alerts can fire 2–4 days after a page changes. During an active deal where a competitor drops their Starter price, that lag means customers are comparing against stale numbers.
